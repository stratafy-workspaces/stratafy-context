## Content
# Core Product Capabilities

The user-facing product surfaces that customers interact with daily. While Infrastructure builds the engine and Intelligence makes it smart, this strategy owns the product areas people actually use. These are organised by the distinct user jobs they serve.

## 1. Strategic Architecture

The system for defining and structuring strategy — the hierarchy of strategies, initiatives, objectives, and plans. This is where leadership answers "what are we doing and why" and builds the connective tissue between high-level intent and operational execution. Full CRUD across all entity types with rich detail views, content editing, and financial model attachments per strategy.

## 2. Strategic Visualisation

Multiple lenses onto the same strategic data, each designed for a different thinking mode. The Strategy Canvas provides an interactive tree for board-level presentations. The Graph view renders network relationships via Vue Flow for systems thinking. The Matrix cross-references strategy against org and finance axes. Horizons, cards, and list views round out the set — same data, different cognitive frames.

## 3. Organisational Foundation

The identity and structure layer. Mission, vision, values, beliefs, and principles define who the organisation is and what it stands for. Teams, positions, and org proposals define how it's structured. This layer connects people to strategy — team ownership, cost modelling via positions, and reports-to hierarchies through org proposals. Includes MVV alignment analytics.

## 4. Financial Intelligence

Connecting money to strategic intent. The Chart of Accounts provides hierarchical account trees with health checks. Financial proposals model budget line items. Strategy-to-account mappings show where spend flows relative to strategic priorities. The AI-driven financial model engine supports KaTeX rendering and natural language editing, making financial planning conversational rather than spreadsheet-bound.

## 5. Strategic Intelligence

The sensing and learning system. Signals capture what's happening in the environment. Insights synthesise patterns from signals. Decisions track the choices made in response. Radar scans run AI-powered environmental sweeps with configurable prompts and sessions. Alignment scans measure coherence across the strategy. Together, this is how the outside world and internal performance feed back into strategic thinking.

## 6. Knowledge & Documents

The workspace's institutional memory. Documents with full versioning, content editing, and a tree-based organisation structure. This is where strategic thinking gets written down, formalised, and made referenceable. Documents can be linked to any entity, creating a knowledge graph that enriches AI context across the platform.

## 7. Metrics & Measurement

Tracking what matters. Metrics with full CRUD, historical value recording, and trend analysis. Connected to objectives and strategies to answer "are we on track." The measurement layer that grounds strategic intent in observable reality.

## 8. AI & Conversation

The AI capability woven through every product area. An embedded chat panel available on any workspace page, pre-loaded with page context — the current entity, related data, and workspace state. Supports natural language queries, financial model editing, scenario exploration, and strategic analysis. Context profiles and MCP tools extend this to external AI clients. Not a destination — a capability layer.

## 9. Collaboration Tools

How people work together inside the platform. InsightSync provides structured workshop sessions with prompts, participant management, and AI-powered analysis of responses. Team management handles workspace membership and roles. Chat provides real-time communication. Partners enables external collaboration. These tools turn Stratafy from a planning tool into a working environment.
