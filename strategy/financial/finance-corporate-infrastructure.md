## Strategic Intent

Build a clean, compliant, and investor-ready financial and corporate infrastructure that supports Stratafy from pre-seed through seed raise — with properly documented IP ownership, a defensible inter-entity structure, and accounting systems that can withstand institutional due diligence.

## Entity Structure

Stratafy operates across two entities, by design:

**Cortex Innovations FZ-LLC (UAE / Sharjah free zone)** is the primary IP-holding entity. WIO business bank account with AED and USD, both linked to Zoho Books. Default COA in place — not yet customised for Stratafy. Currently owned 50/50 between Leonard Cremer and Jacques (silent co-owner with no claim on Stratafy IP — understood verbally, not yet documented).

**Uniteam Investments (SA Pty Ltd)** is a clean, established consulting entity with an Investec bank account. Not VAT registered. Invoicing vehicle for SA clients (STIR Collective, Neopak) in ZAR. Has a deregistration issue to resolve — not a blocker to operations.

## CFC Protection Rationale

Leonard is a South African tax resident. SARS has a worldwide income view. If a SA resident owns more than 50% of a foreign entity, SARS can attribute that entity's income directly to the SA resident under the Controlled Foreign Company (CFC) rules (Section 9D of the Income Tax Act). The 50/50 Cortex shareholding keeps Leonard below this threshold — intentionally.

Jacques' 50% stake will be secured via a **share call option agreement** giving Leonard the right to purchase Jacques' shares at any future date at nominal value, directing the transfer to any company or trust nominated by Leonard (without requiring Jacques' approval — only notification). This preserves CFC protection while securing full operational control.

**Trust destination for Jacques' shares:**
- Path A: Rob Horton's Cyprus trust (preferred — 5-year EO forum relationship, existing international structure)
- Path B: Alternative trust structure if Path A is not viable
- Decision to be made within 60 days. Option is exercised when Delaware migration is triggered.

## Pre-Raise Delaware Migration

When institutional investor conversations become serious, the full structure migrates to a **Delaware C-Corporation**:
- IP assigned from Cortex to Delaware
- Call option exercised — Jacques' shares transferred to nominated trust/entity
- Uniteam enters management services agreement with Delaware
- Leonard invoices Delaware for founder services through Cortex (preferred) or Uniteam — not as a direct US employee
- Delaware becomes the clean cap table entity for Madrona/Sequoia

**Trigger:** First serious institutional investor conversation — not a calendar date. Given April 2026 pitch window, this could be imminent. The Delaware migration is estimated at 60–90 days and $5,000–$15,000 in legal fees.

## Founder Compensation

Leonard is not drawing a salary at pre-seed. Post-raise: Leonard invoices Delaware for consulting/management fees through Cortex Innovations. No direct US employment. This avoids US payroll tax, employment law, and benefits obligations. Standard structure for non-US founders at seed stage.
