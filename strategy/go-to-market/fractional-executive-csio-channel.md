# Fractional Executive & CSIO Channel

## Strategic Intent

**Question answered**: How do we turn fractional executives into a scalable distribution channel for Stratafy?

**Answer**: We position Stratafy as the operating environment that makes fractional executives more valuable — not a replacement, but an amplifier. Each practitioner who adopts Stratafy becomes a channel that brings the product into every client engagement. The best of them evolve into CSIOs: a new board-level role that oversees strategic intelligence across human and AI executives.

**Time Horizon**: Q2 2026 → Q1 2027 (medium-term)

**Strategic Theme**: Platform sale follows role adoption, not software purchase. Win the practitioner first, and the client companies follow.

---

## Why This Channel, Why Now

Fractional executives are the fastest-growing segment in the professional services market. South Africa alone has thousands of independent fractional CFOs, COOs, and CMOs — and the model is exploding globally post-COVID.

These practitioners share a universal pain: **context decay**. They work with 3-5 clients simultaneously. Between sessions, strategic context evaporates. They spend the first 30 minutes of every engagement re-reading notes and asking "where were we?" Stratafy solves this immediately — the AI experts monitor between sessions, the workspace retains full strategic context, and the practitioner walks in already briefed.

This is not a hypothetical value proposition. The moment a fractional CFO sees `/catch-me-up` pull their client's strategic context in seconds, the product sells itself.

**Why distinct from Advisory Firms**: Advisory firms are methodology-led — they sell a framework and Stratafy embeds it. Fractional executives are practitioner-led — they sell their own expertise and Stratafy amplifies it. Different buyer psychology, different acquisition motion, different commercial model.

## Target Persona

### Primary: Independent Fractional Executive

- Solo or small-practice fractional CFOs, COOs, CMOs
- 3-5 concurrent client engagements
- Revenue R50-150k/month across clients
- Pain: context switching, manual reporting, can't scale beyond 5 clients without quality dropping
- Motivation: serve more clients without burning out, deliver better work, justify premium rates

### Secondary: Boutique Fractional Firms

- 5-15 fractional executives under one brand (e.g., Outsourced CFO, CFO SA)
- Pain: inconsistent quality across practitioners, no shared methodology infrastructure
- Motivation: standardise delivery, onboard junior practitioners faster, differentiate vs. independents

### Tertiary: PE Portfolio Operating Partners

- Not fractional executives themselves, but they deploy them
- Pain: inconsistent strategic reporting across portfolio companies
- Motivation: standardised strategic operating layer across 10-50 portfolio companies

## The Pitch

### For Independent Fractional Executives

**"Your clients get an AI expert team. You become the person who makes it all work."**

The core value proposition is layered:

1. **Immediate** — Solve context decay. Walk into every session already briefed. `/catch-me-up` across all your clients in 5 minutes instead of 30.
2. **Near-term** — Deliver more. The Stratafy FD handles monitoring, analysis, and reporting between your sessions. You handle the judgment calls, the board conversations, the things that require a human in the room.
3. **Transformative** — Scale beyond 5 clients. With Stratafy Experts handling the continuous monitoring, you can serve 8-10 clients without quality degradation.
4. **Career** — Evolve into a CSIO. You're not just a fractional CFO anymore — you're the person who oversees an entire AI-powered strategic intelligence function.

### For Boutique Firms

**"Give every practitioner in your firm the same strategic operating layer."**

Standardised delivery. Faster onboarding for junior practitioners. A Stratafy Expert roster that enforces your methodology across all client engagements.

## The CSIO Role

The Chief Strategic Intelligence Officer is a new role that emerges naturally from this channel. It's the human who oversees the full Stratafy Expert roster for a client company — ensuring the AI experts are operating correctly, escalations are handled, and strategic coherence is maintained.

**Evolution path for a fractional executive → CSIO:**

1. **Practitioner** — Uses Stratafy as a personal operating tool across clients
2. **AI-Capable Operator** — Configures and oversees Stratafy Experts for each client
3. **CSIO** — Becomes the recognised board-level role responsible for the strategic intelligence function
4. **CSIO Network** — PE firms deploy certified CSIOs across portfolio companies

The CSIO role is Stratafy's ultimate channel lock-in. Once a company has a CSIO overseeing their expert roster, switching costs become very high — not because of data lock-in, but because the role itself is defined by the platform.

**CSIO certification** is a future initiative (Q4 2026+). For now, the role emerges organically from how practitioners use the product.

## Commercial Model

### For Independent Practitioners

The fractional executive doesn't pay — their client does. The practitioner is the channel, not the customer.

| Model | How It Works | Stratafy Revenue |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Practitioner recommends** | Fractional exec recommends Stratafy to client, client subscribes directly | Full subscription (R8-25k/month) |
| **Practitioner bundles** | Fractional exec includes Stratafy in their retainer, marks up 20-30% | Wholesale rate (~70% of list) |
| **Practitioner resells** | Fractional exec becomes a reseller with ongoing revenue share | 70% to Stratafy, 30% to practitioner |

**Preferred model**: Practitioner bundles. The client sees one invoice from their fractional CFO. Stratafy is embedded in the service, not sold separately. This maximises stickiness and minimises procurement friction.

### For Boutique Firms

Firm-level agreement with volume pricing. The firm deploys Stratafy across all practitioners and all client engagements. Revenue share on client subscriptions.

## Onboarding Journey

**Week 1**: Fractional exec gets personal Stratafy workspace. Learns `/catch-me-up`, `/lets-go`, `/call-it-a-day`. Experiences the context-retention value with their own strategy.

**Week 2-3**: Deploys Stratafy for their highest-trust client. Sets up the client workspace, configures the relevant expert (usually FD). Runs first `/financial-scan` or `/strategy-review` with the client.

**Month 2**: Expands to 2-3 clients. Starts using cross-client patterns ("this worked for Client A, let me try it with Client B"). Begins to see the scale benefit.

**Month 3+**: Full adoption. All clients on Stratafy. Practitioner is now operating as a de facto CSIO — overseeing AI experts across multiple engagements.

## Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Fractional executives see Stratafy as a threat, not an amplifier | Medium | High | Lead with "amplifier" messaging. Show human+AI is more valuable than either alone. |
| Practitioners adopt but don't bring it to clients (personal use only) | Medium | Medium | Onboarding journey explicitly designed to move from personal → client deployment by week 2 |
| "Practitioner bundles" model creates pricing confusion | Low-Medium | Medium | Clear wholesale rate structure. Standard bundling agreement template. |
| CSIO role doesn't gain traction — seen as made-up title | Medium | Low | Role emerges from practice, not from marketing. Don't push the title until practitioners are already doing the job. |
| Channel conflicts with Advisory Firms channel | Low | Medium | Clear delineation: advisory = methodology-led, fractional = practitioner-led. Different buyer, different motion. |

## Strategic Constraints

- This channel MUST NOT require enterprise sales motions — practitioners buy like individuals, not enterprises
- The product experience for the practitioner must work before we attempt client deployment
- CSIO positioning follows adoption, never leads it — don't sell the role, let it emerge
- Commercial model must not create friction between the practitioner and their client
- Product scope (plugin architecture, expert roster) stays in Stratafy Experts — this strategy owns the channel mechanics only
