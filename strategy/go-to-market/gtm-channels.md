# GTM Channels

## Strategic Intent: Parallel Exploration, Not Parallel Execution

This is an **active exploration portfolio** — we are running lightweight probes across multiple distribution channels simultaneously to discover where product-market fit signal is strongest. Every channel conversation shapes the product in real time while generating proof points that compound into a stronger investment story.

The goal is **not** to execute all channels at full capacity. It is to learn from all of them at once, identify which channels pull hardest, and use that evidence to prioritise post-fundraise. Early traction across a few channels demonstrates market breadth and de-risks the GTM thesis for investors.

### Why explore in parallel?

- Each channel surfaces different product requirements — coaches need methodology templates, VCs need portfolio views, fractional execs need onboarding flows. These conversations are shaping the product as we go.
- Proof points across multiple channels are more compelling than deep traction in one. An investor story of "coaches are using it, VCs want portfolio visibility, and advisory firms see it as their operating system" beats "one advisory firm is piloting."
- Early signals help us sequence post-fundraise execution — we'll know which channels to double down on with a team.

## Channel Portfolio

### Tier 1: Active engagement with real conversations
1. **VC Firms** (Madrona, Sequoia, Knife Capital, Clickatell/Pieter) — deploy to portfolio companies, gain portfolio visibility. Dual value as customer + investor path.
2. **Advisory Firms** (ScaleUp) — deploy to clients post-engagement, earn revenue share. First external customer validating the advisory channel.

### Tier 2: Exploratory with emerging signal
3. **Coach Networks** — coaches across Scaling Up, EOS, and other methodology networks. Positioned as additive persistence and visibility tool.
4. **Partner Ecosystem** (We Are 52) — professional services firms building branded plugins on Stratafy infrastructure. HR consulting as the first vertical.
5. **Fractional Executive & CSIO** — practitioners who adopt the platform as their operating environment and bring it into client companies.

### Tier 3: Content-driven, lower-touch
6. **SEO & Category Content** — problem education and category definition content that serves both organic growth and pitch credibility.

## Graduation Criteria

A channel graduates from exploration to committed execution when it demonstrates:
- **Pull signal**: the channel partner actively requests features or next steps without prompting
- **Repeat engagement**: at least 2 independent conversations advance past initial interest
- **Product shaping**: the channel has influenced at least one product decision or feature priority
- **Narrative value**: the channel generates a proof point usable in investor conversations

## Common Value Propositions Across Channels

- Dashboard visibility across multiple companies
- MCP/API access to query across portfolio
- Lock-in through operational embedding
- Network effects as companies interact within the system

## Architecture Fit

Role-Aware Strategic Intelligence supports this natively — same system, different views for VC partner vs portfolio CEO vs advisory firm vs coach vs their clients. The multi-channel exploration is also validating which role contexts matter most.

## Relationship to Product-Market Fit

This strategy operates in service of Product-Market Fit. Every channel interaction is a PMF data point. The exploration runs in parallel with — not ahead of — direct product validation. We respect the principle that direct customers come before channel customers, but we're using channel conversations to learn what direct customers will need.
