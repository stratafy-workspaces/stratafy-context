## Content
# Tax & Compliance

## Objective
Establish a defensible, compliant tax position across SA and UAE entities, with proper cross-border documentation and a clear plan for how the structure evolves at the raise.

## Current State
- Leonard is a South African tax resident — SARS has a worldwide income view
- Cortex Innovations is 50/50 owned — intentionally below the CFC threshold (>50% SA resident ownership triggers CFC attribution)
- No cross-border tax practitioner currently engaged
- No transfer pricing documentation between Uniteam and Cortex
- No formal inter-entity agreement
- Uniteam is not VAT registered (currently below threshold)
- Personal tax position not reviewed in context of cross-border structure

## Sub-Actions

### 3.1 — Engage Cross-Border Tax Practitioner (30 days — MOST CRITICAL DEPENDENCY)
Engage a SA-based tax attorney or specialist firm with active cross-border and trust experience. A standard SA accounting practice is not sufficient — this requires someone who understands CFC rules, transfer pricing, UAE/SA treaty positions, and Delaware migration planning.

Brief must cover:
- Personal tax (SA resident, worldwide income, current structure)
- Uniteam compliance (ITR14 filings, status, any gaps)
- Cortex CFC position (confirm 50/50 structure is properly documented and defensible)
- Inter-entity transfer pricing between Uniteam and Cortex
- Delaware migration planning — tax implications of IP transfer, timing, structure
- Trust structure implications if Rob Horton's Cyprus trust is used

**This practitioner unblocks 2.3, 3.2, and 3.3. It is the highest-priority engagement in this initiative.**

### 3.2 — Document and Defend CFC Position (as part of tax practitioner engagement)
- Confirm 50/50 Cortex structure is properly documented and defensible under SARS CFC rules (Section 9D of the Income Tax Act)
- Understand what happens to CFC position when call option is exercised — if Leonard crosses 50% personally at any point before Delaware migration, income attribution applies
- Document the analysis so it can be produced if SARS queries the structure
- The trust structure (1.3) is the solution — this analysis confirms the urgency of resolving 1.3

### 3.3 — Transfer Pricing Documentation (60 days — requires 3.1)
Document the arm's-length basis for any fees flowing between Uniteam and Cortex:
- What would two unrelated parties agree to for management services / IP licensing?
- This doesn't need to be a complex TP report at this stage — a simple, written, dated document with clear rationale is sufficient for pre-seed
- Must be in place before the raise — investors will ask, and SARS can ask at any time
- **Depends on 3.1 — tax practitioner must advise on the basis**

### 3.4 — VAT Registration Assessment for Uniteam (Quarterly review)
- Current SA client revenue: STIR R25k/month + Neopak (amount TBC) = monitoring required
- Compulsory VAT registration threshold: R1M in any 12-month period
- Voluntary registration: assess whether input tax recovery on business expenses makes this beneficial before hitting the threshold
- **Action:** Review quarterly. Register when approaching R1M or when voluntary registration becomes strategically beneficial

### 3.5 — Personal Tax Review (as part of tax practitioner engagement)
- Ensure Leonard's personal SA tax return correctly reflects worldwide income position
- Confirm no SARS exposure from current Cortex/Uniteam structure
- Plan for how post-raise founder compensation (invoicing Delaware through Cortex or Uniteam) will be treated for SA personal tax purposes
- Leonard is not drawing a salary currently — this simplifies the immediate position but needs to be planned for post-raise

### 3.6 — Founder Compensation Structure Post-Raise (Decision — plan now, implement at raise)
Post-raise structure agreed in principle:
- Leonard invoices Delaware for consulting/management fees through **Cortex Innovations** (preferred) or Uniteam
- No direct employment relationship with Delaware C-Corp
- This avoids US payroll tax, US employment law, and benefits obligations
- Cortex invoicing Delaware is cleaner structurally — keeps the inter-entity flow logical
- Uniteam stays as the SA client invoicing vehicle for ZAR revenue only
- **This decision needs to be confirmed with the cross-border tax practitioner before Delaware incorporation**

## Key Notes
- The cross-border tax practitioner (3.1) is the single most important engagement in this entire strategy — it unblocks at least 5 other sub-actions across initiatives 2 and 3
- The CFC position and transfer pricing documentation are the two items most likely to be queried in investor due diligence — both must be documented before the raise
- The Delaware migration has real tax and timing implications — specifically the IP transfer from Cortex to Delaware may trigger a taxable event. This must be planned carefully with counsel.
