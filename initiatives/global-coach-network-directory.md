## Strategic Rationale

The Global Coach Network solves three problems simultaneously:

**For Stratafy:** Creates a scalable distribution channel where one coach relationship generates many client deployments. Each coach in the network becomes a multiplier — they bring their entire client base onto Stratafy over time.

**For Coaches:** Solves their two biggest pain points — lead generation and client retention. The directory drives inbound leads. The agent-powered intake collapses their sales cycle. The platform makes their methodology sticky with clients.

**For Companies:** Reduces friction in finding the right strategy coach. Instead of cold-searching LinkedIn, they find a curated network with rich profiles. The AI agent does initial assessment work, so the first coach interaction is already contextualized.

## Coach Qualification Gate — "Use It Before You Sell It"

Before a coach can be listed in the public directory and receive inbound leads, they must complete a structured onboarding period operating Stratafy for their own practice. This is non-negotiable.

### Why This Gate Exists

1. **Authenticity**: A coach who hasn't used Stratafy for their own strategy cannot authentically guide clients through it. The directory promises elite coaches — delivering coaches who don't deeply understand the platform erodes trust with both prospects and the network.

2. **Product Validation**: Coaches running their own strategy through Stratafy surface friction, gaps, and feature needs that no survey or interview can capture. Their own practice is at stake — they'll be brutally honest about what works and what doesn't.

3. **Natural Filter**: Coaches who aren't serious won't complete the onboarding period. This self-selects for committed partners and keeps directory quality high. Better to have 20 deeply qualified coaches than 50 surface-level ones.

4. **Sales Competence**: A coach who has lived inside Stratafy for 30+ days can walk a prospect through it with real conviction. They've felt the alignment scans, watched insights compound, experienced the strategic advisor. They're not pitching features — they're sharing experience.

5. **Dogfooding Alignment**: This mirrors Stratafy's own principle — we use our product before we sell it. Extending this to coaches keeps the ecosystem honest.

### Qualification Requirements

**Phase 1 — Own Strategy (Weeks 1-2)**
- Complete workspace setup: foundation (mission, vision, values), strategy tree, at least 3 initiatives with objectives
- Run at least 1 alignment scan on their own strategy
- Log at least 3 decisions or assumptions
- Configure their strategic advisor agent

**Phase 2 — Active Operation (Weeks 3-4)**
- Use Stratafy for daily/weekly strategic work (minimum 3 sessions per week)
- Capture at least 5 insights from their coaching practice
- Update strategy based on real developments (prove the living strategy concept)
- Complete at least 1 strategy review cycle

**Phase 3 — Certification & Listing (Week 5)**
- Brief review call with Stratafy team to discuss experience, feedback, and readiness
- Coach profile finalized and published to directory
- First call playbook reviewed and customized for their methodology
- "Stratafy Certified" badge activated on profile

**Total time to directory listing: ~5 weeks from onboarding start**

### How This Shapes the Timeline

This gate means the Phase 1 target of "10 founding coaches by April" needs to account for the 5-week qualification period. Coaches who start onboarding in early March won't be directory-listed until mid-April. This is acceptable — quality over speed.

For Phase 2 city-by-city expansion, the qualification gate actually helps the FOMO mechanics: "We're onboarding founding coaches in [city] now — the qualification process takes 5 weeks, so the sooner you start, the sooner you're listed and receiving leads." The gate creates additional urgency.

### Exceptions

None for Phase 1. Every founding coach goes through the full gate. In Phase 3 at scale, consider a streamlined path for coaches with verifiable Stratafy experience (e.g., referred by an active network coach who can vouch for their platform competence).

## The FOMO Playbook

1. **Scarcity Signal**: "2-3 founding coaches per city" — limited supply creates urgency
2. **Social Proof**: Public map showing claimed cities creates bandwagon effect
3. **Loss Aversion**: "Coaches in your city are already expressing interest"
4. **Permanent Advantage**: Founding Partner benefits that expire — act now or miss the window
5. **Peer Pressure**: When one Scaling Up coach joins, others in the network hear about it
6. **Revenue Fear**: "Other coaches in your city will be getting the inbound leads"

## Agent-Powered Differentiation

No other directory offers this. Traditional: Company finds coach → emails → schedules call → coach qualifies. Stratafy: Company enters URL → agent researches → workspace created → coach receives pre-researched lead with demo-ready workspace → first call is a strategic conversation.

### Agent Quality Floor
Not every lead will have rich public data. Define minimum acceptable quality thresholds:
- **Auto-send to coach**: Company name, size estimate, industry, 2+ identified challenges, workspace scaffolded with methodology template
- **Manual review required**: Only basic company info found, low confidence on challenges or methodology fit
- **Reject/request more info**: Insufficient data to create meaningful brief (e.g., personal email with no company URL)

Sending a bad brief is worse than sending nothing. Coach trust in the agent's output is the entire value proposition — protect it ruthlessly.

## Coach Success Enablement

Getting coaches into the network is only half the job. The gap between "coach receives a lead" and "coach converts that lead" needs explicit support:

- **First Call Playbook**: How to walk a prospect through the pre-built workspace, what to highlight, how to transition from "impressed" to "signed up"
- **Demo Script Templates**: Methodology-specific scripts for using the workspace as a selling tool (e.g., "Here's your OPSP mapped out — let me show you where the gaps are")
- **Conversion Tracking**: Help coaches see their funnel — leads received, calls booked, engagements started — so they can optimize
- **Peer Learning**: Monthly calls where high-performing coaches share what's working, creating community and raising the conversion bar

This is a Phase 1 deliverable, not Phase 3. Without it, the directory generates leads that don't convert and coaches blame the platform.

## Coach-to-Coach Competitive Dynamics

With 2-3 coaches per city, potentially from the same methodology, lead routing matters:

- **Phase 1**: Manual matching based on best methodology/industry fit
- **Phase 2**: Algorithm-based routing considering methodology match, industry specialization, coach rating, response time, and current capacity
- **Phase 3**: Coach marketplace where prospects can browse matched coaches and choose, with Stratafy's recommendation highlighted

Design principle: make the routing feel fair and merit-based. Coaches who respond faster, convert better, and get higher client satisfaction should get more leads. This creates positive competitive pressure without feeling arbitrary.

## Coach Churn & Data Ownership

Design this upfront to avoid trust issues:

- **Client workspaces persist on Stratafy regardless of coach relationship** — this is the lock-in. If a coach leaves, their clients keep their strategic architecture.
- **Coaches can export their own data** (profile, analytics) but not client workspace data they didn't create
- **Client workspace ownership belongs to the client company**, not the coach. Coaches have advisory access that can be revoked.
- **Transition protocol**: If a coach leaves, their clients are offered connection to another network coach or can continue self-service

Communicate this transparently from day one. Coaches who understand this model will actually value it — their work persists and compounds even across coach transitions.

## Methodology Network Politics

Scaling Up, EOS, and others have their own ecosystems and territorial dynamics. Strategy for methodology HQs:

- **Position as complementary, not competitive**: "We make your methodology persist and measurable between sessions — we're the operating system, you're the methodology"
- **Seek informal blessing before formal partnership**: Start by having coaches demonstrate value to clients, then approach HQ with evidence
- **Avoid using methodology trademarks in ways that imply official endorsement** until partnerships are formalized
- **Monitor for IP concerns**: If EOS Worldwide or Scaling Up HQ views Stratafy as unauthorized use, have a response ready — pivot to "methodology-agnostic strategic operating system" framing if needed

A cease-and-desist at Phase 2 would be catastrophic. De-risk this in Phase 1 through informal conversations with methodology leaders.

## Defensibility: Directory + Intelligence = Moat

The directory alone is replicable. If this works, what stops Scaling Up HQ or EOS Worldwide from building their own version? The answer is the strategic intelligence layer:

- **Directory** = acquisition layer (how coaches and companies find each other)
- **Intelligence** = retention layer (cross-client pattern recognition, compounding insights, agent context that gets smarter with every engagement)

The directory without intelligence is a listing site. The intelligence without the directory has no distribution. Together, they create something no methodology HQ or competing platform can replicate without years of accumulated strategic data across hundreds of client workspaces.

This dependency on the Coach Portfolio Intelligence Platform initiative must be explicitly managed — the intelligence features need to ship in parallel with Phase 2 directory expansion, not after.

## International Operations

The network spans multiple jurisdictions from day one. Design for this early:

- **Multi-currency**: USD, ZAR, AUD, GBP, EUR — use Stripe or similar with automatic conversion
- **Tax compliance**: VAT in EU/UK, GST in Australia, VAT in SA — build tax handling into billing from Phase 1
- **Revenue share accounting**: Cross-border payments to coaches in their local currency
- **Time zone handling**: Notification timing, lead routing, and "business hours" awareness
- **Data residency**: GDPR compliance for EU coaches and prospects, POPIA for South Africa

Architecture these into the platform from the start. Retrofitting international compliance is orders of magnitude harder than building it in.

## Retention Through Indispensability (Not Contracts)

Revenue safeguards against coaches bypassing Stratafy should come from value, not enforcement:

- The workspace IS the product — without it, the coach loses session prep briefs, client retention tools, the intelligence layer
- Make the bypass costly by making the platform indispensable: a coach who takes a client off-platform loses all the accumulated strategic context
- Track behavioral data over surveys: which coaches actively use workspaces, which leads convert, which profiles get views — this honest data drives iteration priority
