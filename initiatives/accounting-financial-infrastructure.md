## Content
# Accounting & Financial Infrastructure

## Objective
Establish a clean, investor-ready accounting infrastructure across both entities that supports accurate reporting, inter-entity documentation, and due diligence readiness.

## Current State
- **Cortex Innovations:** WIO bank account with AED and USD accounts, both linked to Zoho Books. Default Zoho COA in place — not customised for Stratafy's business model (SaaS, IP licensing, R&D, inter-entity transactions).
- **Uniteam Investments:** Investec bank account, not linked to Zoho Books, not VAT registered.
- **Inter-entity flows:** STIR Collective (R25k/month) and Neopak invoiced through Uniteam in ZAR. International/USD revenue through Cortex. No formal inter-entity agreement or transfer pricing documentation in place.
- **Reporting:** No formal monthly close process, no investor-ready reporting pack.

## Sub-Actions

### 2.1 — Rebuild Zoho Books Chart of Accounts for Stratafy (45 days)
Restructure the default COA to reflect Stratafy's actual business model:
- **Revenue accounts:** SaaS subscriptions (ZAR), SaaS subscriptions (USD), IP licensing fees, consulting/retainer revenue, management fees received
- **Cost of revenue:** AI API costs (Anthropic), hosting/infrastructure, customer success
- **R&D expenses:** Product development, MCP tooling, technical infrastructure
- **Inter-entity accounts:** Management fees paid/received, inter-company loans
- **G&A:** Legal, accounting, compliance, bank charges
- Structure should match what an investor or auditor would expect to see for a pre-seed SaaS company

### 2.2 — Establish Clean Invoice Flows by Entity (Immediate)
- **Uniteam Investments** invoices all SA clients (STIR Collective, Neopak) in ZAR
- **Cortex Innovations** invoices all international clients in USD/AED
- All invoices go out under the correct entity from next invoice cycle
- Ensure Zoho Books captures both flows — Cortex transactions in Zoho, Uniteam tracked separately (Investec bank statements at minimum)

### 2.3 — Inter-Entity Management Services Agreement (60 days)
Formal written agreement between Uniteam and Cortex covering:
- Management fees flowing from Uniteam to Cortex for use of Stratafy IP and platform
- Transfer pricing basis — must be arm's-length and defensible under SARS scrutiny
- This is the document SARS would request if they query the structure
- **Requires cross-border tax practitioner input before drafting** — this is a dependency

### 2.4 — Establish Monthly Close Process (Start next month-end)
Basic but consistent monthly close:
- Reconcile WIO (AED and USD) accounts monthly in Zoho Books
- Track Uniteam Investec transactions separately (even if not in Zoho)
- Monthly P&L and balance sheet reviewed by Leonard
- Simple tracker: revenue, burn, runway in months
- This doesn't need to be sophisticated — it needs to be consistent

### 2.5 — Investor-Ready Reporting Pack (90 days)
Build the reporting pack that seed investors expect to see:
- **P&L:** Monthly actuals vs prior month, YTD
- **Cash flow:** Monthly actual, 6-month forward projection
- **ARR bridge:** Starting ARR, new ARR, churn, ending ARR
- **Burn rate:** Monthly burn, runway in months at current burn
- **Unit economics:** CAC, LTV, payback period as these become available
- Build in Zoho Books or linked Google Sheet — must be reproducible monthly with minimal effort

## Key Notes
- Uniteam is not VAT registered. STIR + Neopak = ~R300k/year — below the R1M compulsory threshold. Monitor as revenue scales. Reassess voluntary registration when approaching threshold or when input tax recovery becomes meaningful.
- USD account at WIO is live and linked to Zoho — no new banking infrastructure needed for international revenue
- The COA rebuild is a dependency for meaningful investor-ready reporting
