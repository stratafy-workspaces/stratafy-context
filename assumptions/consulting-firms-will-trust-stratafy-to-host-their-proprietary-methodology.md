# Consulting firms will trust Stratafy to host their proprietary methodology

## Description
The partner model only works if firms are willing to hand over their methodology documents and frameworks for hosting inside Stratafy's infrastructure. Many boutique consultancies treat this IP as their most sensitive competitive asset. They need to be convinced that Stratafy is a custodian, not an owner — and that their IP is technically isolated from other partners and from Stratafy itself.

## Assessment
- **Confidence**: likely
- **Type**: market
- **Impact if Wrong**: critical
- **Status**: active

## Validation
- **Method**: Conduct structured conversations with 3-5 HR consulting principals. Test willingness with a proposed custodianship agreement and technical isolation explainer. Measure response to the IP hosting framing vs self-hosted alternative.
- **Validated At**: Not yet validated
- **Evidence**: Strety/EOS precedent (Jan 2025) proves methodology HQs will license IP to third-party platforms — EOS Worldwide officially licensed their full trademarked toolset with a 20% revenue share model. AI-native delivery (methodology as markdown skill files) lowers perceived IP sensitivity while increasing platform value. Scaling Up's open-architecture model suggests methodology-flexible firms are receptive. However, multi-tenant SaaS security research shows standard logical isolation is insufficient for IP-sensitive partners — BYOK or dedicated isolation may be required to close the trust gap. Net: model is proven viable, but architectural trust measures are a prerequisite, not optional.
