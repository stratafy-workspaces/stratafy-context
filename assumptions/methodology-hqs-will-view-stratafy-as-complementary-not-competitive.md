# Methodology HQs will view Stratafy as complementary, not competitive

## Description
Methodology organizations (Scaling Up, EOS Worldwide) will view Stratafy as complementary to their ecosystem rather than competitive or unauthorized. The positioning as persistence layer for their methodology must land correctly — if they see it as trying to replace or commoditize their IP, the relationship turns adversarial.

## Assessment
- **Confidence**: likely
- **Type**: strategic
- **Impact if Wrong**: critical
- **Status**: active

## Validation
- **Method**: Initiate informal conversations with methodology leaders during Phase 1. Gauge receptivity before scaling to Phase 2. If hostile, test methodology-agnostic positioning before expanding.
- **Validated At**: Not yet validated
- **Evidence**: Research reveals a split picture by methodology type. Open-architecture systems (Scaling Up) are structurally predisposed to view complementary platforms favourably — coaches can already bring third-party tools, so a persistence layer is a natural extension. Closed-architecture systems (EOS) default to adversarial posture — EOS sent Strety a C&D and initially refused licensing. However, the Strety precedent proves even closed systems can be moved: Strety went from C&D to one of only two licensed EOS software partners by building coach adoption first, then getting HQ blessing. The critical implication: Stratafy should lead with open-architecture methodology partners (Scaling Up) and build bottom-up coach adoption before approaching closed-system HQs. The 'persistence layer' framing is key — Stratafy must be positioned as infrastructure beneath the methodology, not a replacement for it.
