# Strategic engagement signals (who viewed, shared, ignored what) predict alignment gaps

## Description
Aligned's engagement analytics (page views, internal forwards, stalled steps) proved that transparency of activity reveals hidden stakeholders and risks that traditional reporting misses. The strategic equivalent: tracking how teams and agents engage with OKRs, initiatives, assumptions, and decisions will surface quiet resistance, hidden champions, and strategic drift before they become visible in quarterly reviews. This is the "buy-in signal" thesis. If wrong — if engagement analytics don't meaningfully predict strategic outcomes — the feature becomes noise rather than signal and erodes trust in the platform.

## Assessment
- **Confidence**: likely
- **Type**: market
- **Impact if Wrong**: medium
- **Status**: active

## Validation
- **Method**: Build engagement tracking into STIR workspace. After 4-6 weeks, correlate engagement patterns with actual strategic outcomes. Do initiatives with low engagement signals perform worse? Do unread assumption updates correlate with misalignment in reviews? If engagement signals predict nothing actionable, deprioritize this feature path.
