# Operational lock-in creates durable switching costs within 8 weeks

## Description
Combined thesis: (1) Once an organization's strategy lives in Stratafy and agents consume it continuously, switching means unwinding how the org operates — not just exporting data. (2) This lock-in materializes within ~8 weeks of active use, not months or years. (3) The resulting moat is stronger than data lock-in or feature differentiation because it's operational dependency, not just data gravity. Needs empirical validation from STIR and ScaleUp before Sequoia pitch. Key questions: Does strategic context compound measurably? Do users become dependent on the flywheel? Would switching feel like a transformation they'd have to undo? If organizations can easily replicate their strategic workflows in another tool or revert to slides and spreadsheets without significant friction, the moat is weaker than theorized and retention depends on continuous feature superiority instead.

## Assessment
- **Confidence**: hypothesis
- **Type**: strategic
- **Impact if Wrong**: critical
- **Status**: active

## Validation
- **Method**: Track STIR flywheel metrics weekly from Feb 17. Chart context depth, insight count, agent sessions, and decisions logged. Ask lighthouse customers directly: "if Stratafy disappeared tomorrow, what would break?" If the answer is "nothing much," the lock-in thesis is failing. Secondary: same metrics for ScaleUp from Feb 23. Tertiary: Keet van Zyl feedback on whether portfolio-level lock-in resonates as a VC.
