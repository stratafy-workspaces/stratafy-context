# Organizations will pay for strategic OS separate from PM/BI/collaboration

## Description
Companies will allocate budget specifically for a strategic operating system as a distinct line item, not bundled into existing PM, BI, or collaboration tool spend. This means willingness-to-pay exists at R25K-100K/month for strategic infrastructure. If wrong, Stratafy competes for existing tool budgets rather than creating new ones, making sales cycles longer and price pressure higher.

## Assessment
- **Confidence**: likely
- **Type**: financial
- **Impact if Wrong**: critical
- **Status**: active

## Validation
- **Method**: Two-stage validation aligned with phased pricing: Phase 1 (Pioneer Program, now–seed close) validates usage and perceived value — do pioneers use weekly, do they say they'd pay? Phase 2 (post-seed) validates willingness-to-pay — do pioneers convert to R25K+ pricing? Target: 70%+ pioneers signal willingness-to-pay at exit survey (Phase 1), then 3+ customers paying from new budget allocation by Q3 2026 (Phase 2).
- **Validated At**: Not yet validated
- **Evidence**: HydraDB prices AI infrastructure at $249–$5,000/mo across startup/growth/enterprise tiers. Zep hit $1M revenue with a 5-person team selling context engineering tools. Mem0 processes 186M API calls/quarter with enterprise customers. STIR Collective paying R25,000/month from new budget allocation (not redirected from existing tool spend) — first direct validation. Market demonstrably pays for AI infrastructure separate from existing tool categories. Three-phase pricing strategy adopted March 2026 to systematically validate at scale.
