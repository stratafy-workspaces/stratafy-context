# Seed raise positioning: Lead with SaaS traction, reveal infrastructure thesis as the "why this is bigger" moment

## Description
How to position Stratafy in seed investor conversations — as a strategy SaaS tool or as strategy infrastructure for the AI era. The product is genuinely both: a workspace where teams build strategy (SaaS surface) AND a platform-neutral data layer that makes every other tool strategy-aware via MCP, API, Chat SDK, and agent context packaging (infrastructure layer). The investor profile shapes expected metrics, growth trajectory, and what kind of company they expect.

## Decision
Lead with tangible SaaS traction (STIR Collective as paying customer, pipeline, ARR trajectory, workspace usage) to establish credibility and urgency. Then expand the aperture to reveal the infrastructure thesis — MCP tools, agent context API, multi-platform Chat SDK distribution, signal pipeline — as the "why this is bigger than it looks" moment. Let investors discover they're investing in strategy infrastructure, not just a strategy tool.

Target investors who understand infrastructure dynamics: Madrona (AI + enterprise infrastructure, existing relationship), Sequoia (infrastructure thesis — Segment, Datadog pattern). Prepare infrastructure-specific metrics alongside SaaS metrics: MCP tool call volume, connected platforms per workspace, agent context requests, signal throughput, API vs direct consumption ratio.

Avoid leading with pure infrastructure narrative at seed — it narrows the pool and risks sounding theoretical without more customer proof points.

## Rationale
Infrastructure companies often look slow early but become deeply embedded and nearly impossible to displace. At seed with one paying customer, leading with infrastructure alone risks skepticism. Leading with SaaS traction provides tangible proof, while the infrastructure reveal demonstrates why the opportunity is 10-100x larger. This mirrors how Segment pitched — started as analytics.js (simple tool), expanded to customer data infrastructure. The dual narrative hedges across investor profiles.
