# Navigation label: "Why Strategies Fail" for Phase 1, "The Execution Gap" reserved for Phase 2/3

## Description
Top-level navigation dropdown label choice: "Why Strategies Fail ▾" vs "The Execution Gap ▾" for website content architecture Phase 1.

## Details
- **Type**: Type 2 (Reversible)
- **Status**: decided
- **Authority**: Not specified
- **Deadline**: No deadline
- **Decided At**: 2026-02-15T09:44:30.674+00:00

## Context
Evaluating top-level navigation label for website content architecture ahead of April 2026 Sequoia pitch window. Need a label that works for cold visitors (no brand awareness), supports SEO, and creates emotional pull. Two candidates: "Why Strategies Fail" (provocative, high-volume search term, self-explanatory) vs "The Execution Gap" (category-defining, proprietary framing, but requires brand awareness to land).

## Research
No research recorded

## Decision
Use "Why Strategies Fail" as the Phase 1 navigation label. Reserve "The Execution Gap" for Phase 2/3 once category awareness is established.

## Rationale
1. Cold visitors understand "Why Strategies Fail" without context — it's a question people actually ask. "The Execution Gap" assumes familiarity with the concept. 2. "Why strategies fail" has higher search volume and maps directly to problem-aware searchers. 3. Question-based framing is emotionally resonant and creates curiosity. 4. "The Execution Gap" is stronger as a category-defining term once Stratafy has enough authority to own it. 5. This is reversible — can transition to "The Execution Gap" in H2 2026 once hub pages rank and category awareness builds.
