# Rename AI executive roster from "The Stratafy Team" to "Stratafy Experts"

## Description
The name "The Stratafy Team" creates collision across three layers: (1) code — teams, team_members, and stratafy_team_members are all distinct entities in the same database, causing ambiguity and future bug risk; (2) product UI — "team" is used for organogram teams (human departments/squads) in the same workspace; (3) sales — "your Stratafy Team" vs "your team" blurs constantly in customer conversations. Options considered: Stratafy Cabinet, Stratafy Executives, Stratafy Council, Stratafy Guild, Stratafy Principals, Stratafy Experts. CMO assessment recommended Stratafy Experts as the strongest choice.

## Research
Options analysed: Cabinet (distinct but formal), Executives (clear but may intimidate SME buyers), Council (premium, no collisions), Guild (memorable but niche), Principals (sharp, VC/law context). Stratafy Experts ruled strongest: honest, collision-free, scalable across buyer sizes and channels, maps cleanly to pricing tier narrative.

## Decision
The AI executive roster product is renamed to Stratafy Experts. In code: stratafy_experts (table/entity). In product UI: Stratafy Experts. In sales: "Your AI experts." The marketing collective noun "The Stratafy Team" may remain in website copy if needed for warmth, but the canonical product entity, code table, and in-product UI all use Experts. Individual experts are referred to as "your FD Expert", "your CSIO Expert" etc.

## Rationale
Experts is honest about what the product delivers — domain-specific AI intelligence, not a team construct. It eliminates all three collision layers simultaneously. It works at every buyer size and channel. It creates a natural upgrade narrative ("which experts does your company need?"). It doesn't collide with advisory firms (a live GTM term), board of directors, or organogram teams. Code entity stratafy_experts is unambiguous. "Your AI expert in finance" is immediately understood by any founder.
