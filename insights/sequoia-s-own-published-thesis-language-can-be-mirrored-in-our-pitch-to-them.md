# Sequoia's own published thesis language can be mirrored in our pitch to them

## Summary
Sequoia's "Services: The New Software" article uses specific frameworks — intelligence vs. judgement, outsourced wedge, disaggregate consulting — that map almost exactly onto what Stratafy does. Using their own language in pitch materials creates instant resonance and positions us as the execution of their thesis.

## Description
Sequoia published a thesis arguing AI will disaggregate services into intelligence components (automatable) and judgement components (human), with the outsourced task as the wedge into larger insourced budgets. Stratafy's positioning maps directly onto this framework: we automate the intelligence layer of strategy work, we target fractional execs and advisory firms as the outsourced wedge, and we leave judgement to human leaders. Reframing our pitch using their exact vocabulary — "intelligence vs. judgement ratio", "outsourced wedge", "disaggregate consulting", "copilot-to-autopilot convergence" — would create immediate recognition and signal that we're building what they've already theorised should exist. This is a rare opportunity to speak a VC's own language back to them with a real product behind it.
