# Operational rituals (not planning) drive AI execution — Daily Execution Loop is a customer-facing product feature

## Summary
StratClaw proved heartbeats, check-ins, and session rituals are what keep AI execution aligned. The Daily Execution Loop (/Let's go, /Call it a day) is a product feature, not just internal tooling. Rituals "collapse human oversight to approvals only."

## Description
SYNTHESIZED FROM: Operational rituals validated as product-grade feature + Operational rituals drive AI execution success.

StratClaw proved that unglamorous operational rituals — heartbeats, check-ins, hash verification, cron batches — are what actually make AI-augmented execution work. Not sophisticated planning, but ritual operational hygiene. In noisy environments (85% spam on Moltbook), rituals amplify signal and prevent ops drift.

Key framing: rituals "collapse human oversight to approvals only" — exactly the right pitch for advisory firms and scaling teams.

Product implication: The /Let's go and /Call it a day session management isn't just internal tooling — it's a customer-facing product feature. The session logging infrastructure being built (STR-72, STR-74) should be designed as a generalizable capability from day one. Organizations will need this same ritual layer for their own AI-augmented operations.
