# Strategic Alignment Score as a trackable KPI creates the retention loop

## Summary
The overall alignment score (8.2/10 for STIR) becomes a board-level KPI when tracked over time. Scan → action → re-scan → show improvement. This is the retention mechanism: customers return to see their score improve. Also provides quantifiable ROI for case studies.

## Description
The STIR report tracks an overall score of 8.2/10 across 7 strategies. When recommendations are actioned and strategies re-scanned, the score should improve. This creates a natural KPI: "Strategic Alignment Score" — a single number that boards, investors, and leadership can track over time. The score trajectory (STR-100) turns the report from a one-time deliverable into a recurring engagement metric. "Your alignment improved from 7.2 to 8.4 after implementing 6 of 10 recommendations" is a quantifiable ROI statement. This is the retention mechanism: customers come back to see their score improve. It also becomes a case study metric: "STIR improved strategic alignment by 15% in first quarter."
