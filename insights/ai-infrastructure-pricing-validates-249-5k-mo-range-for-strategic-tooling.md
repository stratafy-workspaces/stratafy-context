# AI infrastructure pricing validates $249–$5K/mo range for strategic tooling

## Summary
HydraDB prices AI agent infrastructure at $249/mo (startup), $999/mo (growth), and $5,000/mo (enterprise). This suggests the market accepts meaningful pricing for AI-native infrastructure — a useful benchmark for Stratafy's own pricing architecture as a strategic operating system.

## Description
HydraDB (AGI Context, Inc.) has launched with a clear tiered pricing model for AI infrastructure: $249/mo for startups, $999/mo for growth, and $5,000/mo for enterprise — all positioned as infrastructure that replaces vector databases with structured context engineering for AI agents.

This is notable because:

1. **The market is paying for AI infrastructure, not just AI features.** HydraDB isn't selling a chatbot or copilot — it's selling the underlying context layer that makes agents work better. Stratafy occupies a similar structural position for strategy execution.

2. **Enterprise willingness at $5K/mo for a context layer.** If companies will pay $5K/mo for a generic agent memory layer, the willingness-to-pay for a domain-specific strategic intelligence platform (which delivers measurably higher business value) should be at least comparable, if not higher.

3. **Tiered pricing by company stage works for infrastructure.** Their startup→growth→enterprise progression mirrors the natural adoption path Stratafy targets (advisory firms → portfolio companies → direct enterprise).

4. **SOC 2 and GDPR compliance are table stakes at enterprise tier.** This confirms compliance requirements Stratafy will need for enterprise pricing tiers.

Implication: Stratafy's pricing model should confidently target the $500–$5K/mo range for its core platform, with enterprise tiers potentially exceeding $5K/mo given the higher strategic value delivered. The HydraDB benchmark suggests the market won't flinch at infrastructure-level pricing for AI-native tools.
