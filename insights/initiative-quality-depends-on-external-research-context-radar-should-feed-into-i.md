# Initiative quality depends on external research context — Radar should feed into initiative design

## Summary
STIR initiatives were grounded in real competitive data (Gearhouse benchmarks), market sizing, and industry catalysts. Without this research, output would be generic. Future opportunity: Radar findings should inform initiative creation, closing the external intelligence → internal strategy loop.

## Description
Building the STIR workspace required heavy use of external research (STR-70) to ground initiatives in real data: Gearhouse as competitor benchmark (9 subsidiaries, 3 cities, 28 venue contracts, 7K LinkedIn followers), market data (SA events 5.1% CAGR, virtual production $2B by 2030), catalyst events (G20, Cricket WC, F1), and STIR-specific assets (Sharif's industry positions, Level 1 B-BBEE). Without this research, initiatives would have been generic. The quality of strategic output is directly proportional to the quality of contextual input. Future consideration: can the Radar feature feed competitive and market intelligence directly into initiative design? That would close the loop between external intelligence and internal strategy.
