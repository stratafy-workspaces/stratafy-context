# Structured strategy depth is the moat — one-page summaries cause the misalignment

## Summary
Rob validated that one-page strategy summaries are good for buy-in but the lack of detail causes execution misalignment — especially with AI. The depth of structured strategy data is both the value proposition AND the competitive defensibility.

## Description
During the discussion, Rob and Leonard aligned on a critical point: one-page strategy summaries get human buy-in but cause misalignment in execution and with AI agents. The detail that's missing from summaries is exactly what causes drift.

This validates "depth over simplicity" as a deliberate strategic choice for Stratafy. Competitors can build dashboards and summaries, but the structured depth — beliefs with explicit tradeoffs, principles with operational guidance, initiatives linked to objectives linked to metrics — is what makes AI useful AND what's hard to replicate.

The moat is not the UI or even the AI features. The moat is the structured strategy data itself. Once an organization has invested in defining their strategy at this depth, switching costs are enormous.

This reinforces: don't simplify the data model to make onboarding easier. Instead, invest in AI-assisted onboarding that helps organizations BUILD the depth quickly (the workspace setup command demonstrated this).
