# The value of alignment scans is in cross-strategy synthesis, not individual reviews

## Summary
Running 7 individual alignment scans on STIR produced useful but siloed findings. The manual synthesis into a consolidated report revealed systemic patterns (Craft unprotected in 5/7, Skills Dev disconnected in 4/7) that no individual scan surfaced. The report — not the scans — is the product moment.

## Description
During the STIR Collective workspace build-out, we ran 7 comprehensive alignment scans (one per strategy). Each individual scan scored between 7.2 and 8.7 and produced useful findings. But the real intelligence emerged only when we manually synthesized all 7 into a single report.

Three systemic patterns were invisible at the individual scan level:
1. Craft quality unprotected across 5 of 7 strategies — no single scan could see this was systemic
2. Skills Development disconnected from execution in 4 of 7 strategies — each scan noted the gap in its own strategy but couldn't see the pattern
3. "Build don't rent" principle applied inconsistently in 3 of 7 — the principle was well-operationalized in Capability Ownership (8.7) but absent from Brand, Talent, and others

This means the current product architecture (individual scans) captures maybe 40% of the available intelligence. The synthesis layer captures the other 60%. This has direct implications for pricing, positioning, and the demo narrative — the report is what makes CEOs and boards say "how did we miss that?"

This also validates the "Strategic Intelligence Engine" positioning over "alignment tool" — the intelligence is in the pattern recognition across the full strategy tree.
