# Richard Mulholland wants immediate implementation — first urgency signal

## Summary
Richard Mulholland wants to start implementing Stratafy with one of his companies immediately after the EO Forum presentation. Specific pull: daily employee check-ins and continuous strategy updates. This is the first real urgency signal — moving from "interesting" to "I want this now." Meeting scheduled for Monday to discuss.

## Description
Following the EO Forum Deep Dive presentation (March 2026), Richard Mulholland expressed immediate interest in implementing Stratafy with one of his companies. This is the first time someone has moved from interest to "let's do this now" — exactly the urgency gap Leonard identified in the PMF slide.

What specifically pulled Rich:
1. Daily check-ins for each employee — a way to keep every team member connected to the strategy daily, not just at quarterly reviews
2. Continuous strategy updates — the strategy evolves in real-time rather than sitting static in a document

Implementation details (from follow-up conversation):
- Company: Missing Link
- Rich does not run Missing Link day-to-day — his role would be board member / advisor / coach
- They do not have a formal strategy. They use a concept called "victory condition" to align the company continuously
- No existing methodology (OKRs, EOS, etc.) to migrate from — strategy needs to be defined from scratch
- Meeting scheduled for Monday to discuss implementation

This is significant because:
- Rich is a well-known speaker and business leader. If this implementation succeeds, it becomes a powerful case study and reference.
- He's implementing for a TEAM, not as a solo founder. This validates the "Have a team? They're your advisor, assistant, and coach" use case from the Experts slide.
- Rich's role as an external advisor/board member directly validates the "coaches get earlier insight into their clients" insight. He IS that use case — he wants strategic visibility into a company he doesn't operate daily.
- The features he's drawn to (daily check-ins, continuous updates) are about ongoing engagement — high-frequency touchpoints that would drive daily active usage and retention.
- This maps to multiple dragons: post-offsite decay (#2), dead documents (#3), inconsistent story (#5), and new hires start blind (#7). Daily check-ins solve all of these.
- No existing strategy framework means the onboarding starts with strategy definition. This tests whether Stratafy can help a team go from "victory condition" to a structured strategy quickly — directly testing the time-to-define pain point.

Rich is also the person whose framework (Reason to Care → Reason to Believe → What You Need to Know → What You Need to Do) structured the presentation. His buy-in carries extra weight — he understands positioning and wouldn't commit unless the value proposition was clear.

Key questions for Monday meeting:
- How big is the Missing Link team?
- Who runs the day-to-day? (That person is the key operator/champion)
- What does "victory condition" look like today — is it written down somewhere?
- What does Rich want to see as the advisor? (Reports? Dashboards? Weekly digests?)
- What does the team need day-to-day? (Check-ins via Claude? Telegram? Google Chat?)
- What does success look like in 30/60/90 days?
- Pricing and packaging — is this a paid implementation or a design partner arrangement?
