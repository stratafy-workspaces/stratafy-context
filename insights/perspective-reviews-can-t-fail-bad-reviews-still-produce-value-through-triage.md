# Perspective Reviews can't fail — bad reviews still produce value through triage

## Summary
STR-120 (weak Startup Advisor review) still produced value: it confirmed current direction and captured dismissal rationale. This means Perspective Reviews have a non-obvious property — they generate value regardless of AI output quality. Good review = new gaps. Bad review = confirmed direction. Both produce documented strategic reasoning.

## Description
The triage of STR-120 (Startup Advisor — low signal) revealed something unexpected: even the weak review had value. Not because it surfaced new information, but because the triage process confirmed that current strategy is on track. "Yes, we're already doing this" is validation. "No, this doesn't apply to our ICP" is boundary clarification.

This means Perspective Reviews have a non-obvious property: they can't actually fail. A good review surfaces new gaps to address. A bad review confirms existing direction. Both produce value — just different kinds.

This reframes the product guarantee. You don't promise "8 brilliant insights every month." You promise "8 structured stress-tests that either surface what you're missing or confirm what you're doing right — and either way, the rationale is captured."

The captured rationale is the real product:
- Dismissed finding + rationale = documented strategic confidence ("we considered this and here's why it's not relevant")
- Deferred finding + rationale = future awareness ("not now, but we'll revisit when conditions change")  
- Accepted finding + rationale = new strategic work with provenance

This has implications for advisory firms: the triage report itself is a deliverable. A coach can show a client: "We ran 8 perspectives on your strategy. Here's what the virtual CFO found. Here's what the virtual CTO found. Here's what we're acting on, what we're deferring, and what we dismissed with reasons." That's a $5k-$15k strategy health assessment generated in hours, not weeks.

It also addresses a common objection to AI tools: "what if it gives bad advice?" The answer: the advice isn't the product. The structured triage IS the product. Bad advice that gets dismissed with rationale is still valuable.
