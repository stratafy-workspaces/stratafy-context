# Multi-surface access dissolves the change management barrier to enterprise adoption

## Summary
Rob identified that when staff interact through Chat/Telegram/Claude and never open Stratafy, "change control" disappears as an objection. The biggest enterprise adoption risk isn't the product — it's asking people to use another tool. Multi-surface access eliminates this.

## Description
Rob Horton explicitly stated that Stratafy functioning as back-end glue reduces "change control" as an issue. This is a significant PMF signal.

Enterprise software adoption fails most often because of change management — training, onboarding, getting people to log in. If 90% of an organization's interaction with strategy happens through tools they already use (Google Chat, Slack, Telegram, Claude Desktop), the adoption curve flattens dramatically.

This means the browser dashboard is for the strategy team (5-10 people), while the entire organization accesses strategy through their existing workflow. This shapes product priorities: invest in bot/integration quality over dashboard features for end users.

Action: Position multi-surface access as a core differentiator in sales conversations. Lead with "your team never needs to learn a new tool" rather than feature lists.
