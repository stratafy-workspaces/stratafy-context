# CEO strategy pain points: time scarcity, clarity paralysis, and post-offsite decay

## Summary
Rob identified three specific strategy pain points from his own CEO experience: (1) finding the time and right people to dedicate to strategy, (2) gaining clarity on what to actually focus on, (3) post-session exhaustion preventing synthesis into shareable documentation. These are the language real CEOs use.

## Description
Rob Horton, speaking from his own experience as a CEO, identified three specific pain points related to strategy:

1. **Time scarcity** — finding the time and the right people to dedicate to strategy work. It always competes with operational urgency.

2. **Clarity paralysis** — gaining clarity on what to actually focus on. Too many priorities, not enough conviction about which ones matter most.

3. **Post-offsite decay** — the exhaustion following strategy sessions prevents proper synthesis. The offsite generates energy and ideas, but nobody has the bandwidth to turn it into shareable documentation that the rest of the org can use.

These are more specific and emotionally resonant than "70-90% of strategies fail." They're the SYMPTOMS that CEOs actually experience. The statistic is abstract; these pain points are personal.

For the EO presentation: weave these into the Problem section as specific scenarios. "How many of you had a great offsite — and then nothing changed because nobody had time to write it up properly?"

For product positioning: each pain point maps to a Stratafy capability:
- Time scarcity → AI-assisted strategy generation (workspace setup in minutes)
- Clarity paralysis → prioritization tools, alignment scans
- Post-offsite decay → AI synthesizes discussion into structured strategy

Action: Use these three pain points as the primary "reason to care" framing in the EO presentation and test whether they resonate.
