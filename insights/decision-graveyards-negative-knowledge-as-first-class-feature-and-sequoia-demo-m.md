# Decision Graveyards: negative knowledge as first-class feature and Sequoia demo moment

## Summary
Tracking what was rejected and why prevents re-litigation, saves 70-80% of wasted cycles, and is genuinely differentiated. Must be first-class in product and agent context packaging. Prime candidate for Sequoia demo aha moment.

## Description
SYNTHESIZED FROM: Negative knowledge (Decision Graveyards) validated as first-class product feature + Decision Graveyards as uniquely differentiated product feature.

StratClaw's "Decision Graveyard" — tracking rejected decisions with reasoning — emerged as one of the most valuable operational patterns in its 2-week research. Key findings: (1) Negative knowledge is more valuable than positives — avoiding repeated failures prevents 70-80% of wasted cycles. (2) For AI agents, without embedded graveyards, agents re-litigate settled decisions every session. (3) For multi-agent coordination, shared negative knowledge prevents incentive misalignment.

Product implications: Make negative knowledge preservation a first-class feature in Stratafy. When packaging context for agents via the Agent Registry, include not just what to do but what's been explicitly ruled out and why. This is genuinely differentiated — no strategy tool does this well.

Sequoia demo application: Decision Graveyards should be the "aha moment" — every founder and exec has experienced the pain of re-litigating dead decisions. The visceral recognition creates immediate product-market fit signal.
