# Use personal pivot story as proof — "If I'd had this at Troop, here's what would have been different"

## Summary
Rob suggested using Leonard's own experience with a previous business's strategic pivot to demonstrate how Stratafy could have helped define, communicate, and align the organization with the new mission. Personal stories from the founder are more credible than abstract statistics.

## Description
Rob suggested that Leonard use his own experience — building two companies to ~100 people, raising $40M at Troop with Madrona — to ground the presentation in personal credibility.

Specifically: describe a moment where strategy broke down. A pivot that was hard to communicate. A team that lost alignment. Then show how Stratafy would have changed the outcome.

"If I'd had this at Troop when we pivoted, I could have updated the strategy in Stratafy, every team member's AI would have known the new direction by Monday morning, and the alignment scan would have flagged which teams were still executing on the old strategy."

This is more powerful than statistics because:
- It's personal and authentic (EO forums value vulnerability)
- It demonstrates that Leonard built Stratafy to solve his OWN problem
- It's specific and concrete rather than abstract
- Other CEOs in the room have had similar experiences

Action: Develop 2-3 specific "what would have been different" scenarios from Troop or the other business. Use the most compelling one in the Problem section of the EO presentation.
