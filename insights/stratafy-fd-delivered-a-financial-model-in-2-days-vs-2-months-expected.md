# Stratafy FD delivered a financial model in 2 days vs 2 months expected

## Summary
A client expected a detailed financial model to take 2 months and R200,000. Using the Stratafy FD — reading the board pack, building the strategy in Stratafy, and reworking existing models — a first version was delivered in 2 days.

## Description
**What happened:** A client needed a detailed financial model. Their expectation was two months of work at approximately R200,000. Using the Stratafy FD plugin — reading the board pack, building the client's strategy in Stratafy, and reworking their existing models with the Claude Cowork team — a first version was delivered in two days.

**Why this matters strategically:**

1. **Autopilot proof point** — This is the first confirmed instance of the Stratafy FD delivering a professional services outcome, not just answering questions. It directly validates the Sequoia "Services: The New Software" thesis — selling the work, not the tool.

2. **ROI is quantifiable** — Two months vs two days. R200,000 expected vs a fraction of that actual. These are numbers a CEO can immediately translate to their own situation. This is the clearest ROI story Stratafy has.

3. **Assumption validation** — The Stratafy Team strategy document lists the assumption "buyers enter through I need an FD and expand to the full team" at 55% confidence with no data. This is the first data point. A client needed a financial model — they got it through the FD. That's the entry archetype working exactly as designed.

4. **Positioning shift** — The story reframes the Stratafy FD from "AI that helps with finance" to "AI that does the financial work." That's a meaningful upgrade in how the product gets described and sold.

**GTM use:** This story is now Demo 4 in the EO Forum presentation (anonymised). It belongs in investor conversations, sales demos, and any context where the autopilot positioning needs a concrete proof point.

**The one-liner:** "A client expected two months and R200,000. We delivered in two days."
