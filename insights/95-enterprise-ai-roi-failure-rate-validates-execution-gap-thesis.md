# 95% enterprise AI ROI failure rate validates execution gap thesis

## Summary
MIT/PwC/Forrester convergence: 95% zero ROI, only 12% reporting both revenue growth and cost reduction, 25% deferral of 2026 AI spend. Direct pitch ammunition for Sequoia "why now" slide.

## Description
Multiple authoritative sources converging on the same finding: enterprise AI investments are not generating returns. MIT figures show 95% of organizations see zero ROI. PwC reports only 12% of CEOs achieving both revenue growth and cost reductions from AI. Forrester predicts 25% deferral of 2026 AI spend. Kyndryl reports 61% of leaders under pressure to prove ROI with 70-80% of initiatives failing to scale. The common thread: sequencing and scaling issues — exactly the execution gap Stratafy addresses. This reframes the pitch from "spend more on AI" to "stop wasting the AI budget you've already committed." The 95% stat is the most powerful single number for the Sequoia deck's problem slide. Combined with Deloitte's finding that only 15% link AI to profitability, this creates urgency for strategic infrastructure that ensures AI investments actually deliver.
