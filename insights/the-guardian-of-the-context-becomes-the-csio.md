# The Guardian of the Context becomes the CSIO

## Summary
The Guardian of the Context (Rob Horton's buyer archetype) and the CSIO are the same person at different stages of category maturity. We sell to Guardians today; Stratafy transforms them into CSIOs over time.

## Description
The Guardian of the Context and the CSIO are not two different roles — they are the same person at different stages of Stratafy's category maturity.

**The Guardian of the Context** is the buyer archetype today. The founder, COO, or strategic advisor who intuitively understands that the execution gap is an infrastructure problem. They feel the pain most acutely and become the internal champion for Stratafy. They don't yet have a title for what they do.

**The CSIO** is the destination archetype. The role Stratafy creates and names. Initially delivered as an AI plugin, evolving into a recognised C-suite position as the category matures.

**The evolution arc:**
- Today: The Guardian champions Stratafy internally because they feel the context problem acutely
- Next: They install the Stratafy Team, oversee the AI executives, and naturally adopt the CSIO identity
- Later: Companies start hiring for this role explicitly — the Guardian finally has a title

**GTM implication:** We are not selling to CSIOs yet — they don't exist. We are selling to Guardians of the Context, and Stratafy is the thing that transforms them into CSIOs. The Guardian is the entry archetype; the CSIO is the destination archetype.

**Investor implication:** When the first company posts a job listing for Chief Strategic Intelligence Officer without Stratafy prompting it, the category is real. Stratafy created the role, provided the platform, and defined the category — the Salesforce playbook executed.
