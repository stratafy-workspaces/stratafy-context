# Coach-Led Distribution Requires Simplified Messaging and a New Governance Role

## Summary
Coaches are validated as the primary distribution channel, but success depends on a simplified three-step value prop, audience-calibrated messaging that bridges the AI literacy gap, and the emergence of a Guardian of the Context role coaches can sell.

## Description
Four insights form a coherent picture of the coach channel requirements. Coaches are validated — they already do strategy work and Stratafy captures it in structured form, giving them a better deliverable and ongoing relationship. But two barriers must be addressed. First, the AI literacy gap: not all CEOs understand agents and MCP, even if they use ChatGPT for search. A 2-hour workshop converts them, suggesting onboarding is a coach-delivered service. Second, the value prop must be simple enough for coaches to deliver: the three-step model (rapidly generate strategy from context, store as queryable infrastructure, automated feedback replaces alignment meetings) tested better than the 4+3 internal model. The Guardian of the Context role — someone who governs what AI proposes vs what gets accepted — creates a natural organizational champion that coaches can position and train. This directly impacts Coach Network GTM strategy, Category Creation (CSIO role definition), and the pricing model (coaches need to understand and sell pricing — complexity kills channel sales).
