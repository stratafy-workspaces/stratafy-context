# Audience modes as a product feature — same strategy, different story

## Summary
A strategy page with selectable audience modes — Investor, Customer, Partner, Team — each generating a different talk track and flow through the same strategy tree. One click changes the lens, not the data.

## Description
Emerged from a product design session building the strategy storytelling UI. The core observation: the same strategy workspace needs to tell fundamentally different stories depending on who's in the room. An investor cares about the bet, the moat, and the ask. A customer cares about what changes for them. A partner cares about the commercial model. A board cares about execution health.

The current paradigm forces the founder to mentally reframe the same content for every audience — and risks drift between what's said and what's actually in the system. Audience modes solve this by making the workspace itself the presentation layer.

Key product implications:
1. Audience modes are configurations: which nodes matter, in what order, with what narrative framing. That data should live on the strategy node itself as a 'narratives' field, or be AI-generated on demand when an audience is selected.
2. The more powerful version is user-defined flows — not just preset audiences, but named flows saved per meeting. 'Ted Kummert — April pitch', 'We Are 52 — March partner meeting'. Each is a saved sequence of nodes with talk track, shareable via link.
3. This is a direct incentive mechanism for workspace hygiene. The better your workspace, the better your pitch. Strategy maintenance becomes self-reinforcing.
4. The prototype used four audience modes with hardcoded talk tracks. The production version needs AI-generated talk tracks derived from actual workspace data — metrics, initiatives, assumptions — so they stay current automatically.

This feature sits at the intersection of Category Creation and Product-Market Fit: it demonstrates the 'demo is the product' principle live, and is itself a compelling demo hook for every audience type.
