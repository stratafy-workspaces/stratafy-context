# Plugins framed as "a team of AI experts" is more accessible than technical MCP language

## Summary
Rob responded well to framing Stratafy plugins as "a team of AI experts" — Finance Director, Chief of Staff, Team member. This is more accessible than "MCP tools" or "Cowork plugins" for non-technical audiences. Each expert has defined skills and commands.

## Description
During the demo of the Stratafy Team and Finance Director plugins, Rob responded positively to the framing of plugins as team members rather than technical tools.

"A team of AI experts" communicates:
- Each one has a defined role (Finance Director, Chief of Staff)
- Each one has specific skills and capabilities
- They work together and share context
- You can create new experts for specific needs

This is dramatically more accessible than:
- "MCP tools" (meaningless to non-developers)
- "Cowork plugins" (sounds technical)
- "AI agents" (vague and overused)

The Team plugin as the one "everyone in the company uses" vs specialized plugins for specific roles creates a natural hierarchy that maps to how organizations actually work.

For the EO presentation: lead with "Stratafy gives you a team of AI experts who know your strategy" rather than explaining the technical architecture. Show the Finance Director plugin, the Team plugin — let them see the role-based structure.

Action: Adopt "team of AI experts" as the primary framing for plugins in all non-technical conversations. Update sales and presentation materials accordingly.
