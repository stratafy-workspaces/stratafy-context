# Existing strategy methodologies (OKRs, EOS, Scaling Up) feel broken — Stratafy seen as replacement

## Summary
EO Forum members volunteered that methodologies like OKRs, Scaling Up, and EOS aren't working for them, and they see Stratafy as a potential replacement. This is both a strong PMF signal and a positioning risk. The opportunity may be: "Stratafy doesn't replace your methodology — it makes any methodology actually work."

## Description
During the EO Forum Deep Dive (March 2026), several attendees expressed frustration with established strategy methodologies — OKRs, Scaling Up, and EOS were specifically mentioned. They felt these frameworks weren't delivering on their promise. Unprompted, they suggested Stratafy could replace these approaches entirely.

This is significant for several reasons:

1. It validates the core problem — even with well-known, well-documented frameworks, strategy execution still fails. The methodology isn't the bottleneck; the infrastructure to maintain and execute is.

2. It reveals a positioning opportunity — but also a risk:
- Risk: Positioning against OKRs/EOS/Scaling Up means picking a fight with established ecosystems that have consultants, books, communities, and certifications behind them. This could create resistance rather than pull.
- Opportunity: Position Stratafy as the infrastructure that makes ANY methodology work. "OKRs don't fail because OKRs are bad. They fail because there's nothing keeping them alive between quarterly reviews. EOS doesn't fail because the system is wrong. It fails because nobody maintains it between sessions."

3. This is another dragon not on the original list of 9 — "My methodology isn't working." It's distinct from "dead documents" or "post-offsite decay" because it's frustration with the framework itself, not just the execution of it.

Positioning implications:
- "Framework-agnostic" could be a powerful differentiator — Stratafy works with OKRs, EOS, Scaling Up, or your own approach
- The AI experts could be trained to understand and support multiple methodologies
- The structured layers (Foundation → Strategies → Initiatives → Metrics) can accommodate any framework's concepts
- Lead with "the infrastructure that makes your strategy framework actually deliver" rather than "a replacement for your strategy framework"

This connects to the time-to-define insight: part of why these methodologies feel broken is the overhead of maintaining them. Stratafy removes that overhead.
