# Pricing model undefined — traditional SaaS may not fit when most users never open the UI

## Summary
Pricing model discussed but unresolved. Base cost + per-seat or usage-based considered. Traditional per-seat SaaS doesn't fit when 90% of users interact through bots/integrations, not the UI. Usage-based (API calls, agent interactions) may better capture value.

## Description
Rob and Leonard discussed pricing but didn't reach a conclusion. The challenge: traditional per-seat SaaS pricing doesn't map well to Stratafy's usage pattern.

If the dashboard is used by 5-10 people (strategy team) but strategy is consumed by the entire organization through bots and integrations, per-seat pricing either:
- Undercharges (only count dashboard users) and leaves value on the table
- Overcharges (count all employees) and creates adoption friction

Alternative models to explore:
1. Base platform fee + usage-based (API calls, agent interactions, workspace events)
2. Base platform fee + per-workspace pricing (scales with organizational complexity)
3. Tiered by company size with unlimited seats (the "infrastructure" pricing model — like Xero pricing by features, not users)

The pricing model also affects the coach channel — coaches need to understand and sell the pricing. Complexity kills channel sales.

This is a strategic risk. Undefined pricing blocks sales conversations and investor discussions. Need to resolve before serious pipeline development.

Action: Research infrastructure/platform pricing models (Stripe, Twilio, Segment). Test 2-3 models with early customers. Resolve before seed raise.
