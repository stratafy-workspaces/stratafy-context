# Perspective Reviews are the advisory firm's productized service offering

## Summary
Advisory firm coaches ARE human perspective reviewers — they apply expertise lenses to client strategy. Perspective Reviews automate the space between sessions, letting coaches arrive pre-informed. This creates a specific revenue model (setup + subscription + retainer + premium sessions) and should be framed as "what your coaches could offer between sessions" for ScaleUp demo.

## Description
The STR-121 feature spec describes Perspective Reviews as having scheduled, event-triggered, and on-demand modes. But looking at this through the advisory firm lens reveals something more specific: Perspective Reviews are the advisory firm's productized service.

Today, a Scaling Up or EOS coach delivers value through facilitated sessions — essentially applying their expertise lens to the client's strategy. They ARE human perspective reviewers. Their expertise IS a functional lens.

Perspective Reviews automate the space between coaching sessions. The coach runs quarterly deep dives (human judgment, relationship, nuance). Stratafy runs monthly perspective reviews in between (structured, consistent, context-rich). The coach reviews the automated findings before the next session, arrives already knowing what changed and what needs attention.

This creates a specific revenue model for the advisory firm channel:
1. Coach onboards client to Stratafy (setup fee)
2. Monthly automated perspective reviews run continuously (subscription)
3. Coach reviews triage reports, adds human insight (retainer value)
4. Quarterly deep sessions informed by 3 months of automated intelligence (premium)

The coach doesn't lose value — they gain leverage. Instead of spending session time on discovery ("what's changed since we last met?"), they arrive with a pre-triaged findings list and spend session time on the hard judgment calls.

For STIR specifically: this is testable now. Run a manual CFO perspective review on STIR's workspace before the next advisory session. If the findings are useful to whoever is guiding STIR's strategy, that validates the advisory firm channel thesis.

For ScaleUp demo (Feb 23): Frame Perspective Reviews as "what your coaches could offer between sessions." If Nicholas (ScaleUp) sees this as a service his firm could sell, that's channel validation.
