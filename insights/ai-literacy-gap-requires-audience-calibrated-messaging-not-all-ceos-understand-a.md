# AI literacy gap requires audience-calibrated messaging — not all CEOs understand agents

## Summary
Not all CEOs understand AI agents, MCP, or Claude plugins — even if they use ChatGPT for basic search. A 2-hour workshop converts them, suggesting onboarding needs a guided experience, not self-serve. Messaging must calibrate to audience AI literacy.

## Description
Leonard and Rob discussed that a manufacturing CEO might use AI for basic tasks but not understand concepts like AI agents, MCP tools, or Claude plugins. However, a two-hour workshop can quickly convert someone to the value of these tools.

This creates a segmentation challenge:
- AI-native audience (tech founders, developers): lead with infrastructure, MCP, multi-agent
- AI-curious audience (traditional CEOs, coaches): lead with outcomes — "your strategy stays alive, your AI knows your values, your team gets answers without opening another app"

The two-hour conversion window suggests that self-serve onboarding is unlikely to work for the AI-curious segment. They need guided experiences — whether through a coach, a workshop, or a demo-led sales process.

For the EO forum specifically: assume mixed AI literacy. Lead with business outcomes and the Xero/GitHub analogy. Only go technical when someone asks "how does the AI access it?"

Action: Develop two messaging tracks — one for AI-native, one for AI-curious. Ensure the EO presentation works for the AI-curious majority.
