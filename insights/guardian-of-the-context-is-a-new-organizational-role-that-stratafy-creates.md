# "Guardian of the Context" is a new organizational role that Stratafy creates

## Summary
Rob flagged that someone must govern what AI proposes vs what gets accepted into the strategy. This creates a new role — the Guardian of the Context. This person is likely the buyer/champion and shapes the approval workflow product requirements.

## Description
Rob Horton raised a critical concern: if AI agents manage context unchecked, the company's strategy could become completely skewed. Someone needs to be the "guardian of the context" — governing permissions, approving AI-proposed changes, and ensuring accuracy.

This has three implications:

1. Product: Approval workflows, permission tiers, and audit trails are not nice-to-haves — they're core to the value prop. The signal/insight triage prototype addresses this but needs to be robust.

2. GTM: The Guardian of the Context IS the buyer. In small companies it's the CEO/founder. In larger orgs it could be Chief of Staff, Head of Strategy, or COO. Identifying this person early in sales conversations is critical.

3. Positioning: This role already exists informally (someone "owns" the strategy deck). Stratafy formalizes it and gives them tools. This is a selling point, not a burden — "we give the person who already owns your strategy superpowers."

Action: Define the Guardian of the Context persona. Build approval workflows as a priority. Use this role in sales qualification.
