# Consolidated alignment reports are automated consulting deliverables — the monetization mechanism for the advisory GTM channel

## Summary
The STIR alignment report produced what a strategy consulting engagement delivers: prioritized gaps with specific recommendations. For advisory firms, this becomes their branded deliverable. For direct customers, it replaces quarterly strategy reviews. For investors, it quantifies strategic improvement. This is potentially the primary monetization lever.

## Description
The STIR alignment report naturally generated its own action plan — 10 prioritized recommendations that essentially became the first quarter's consulting workplan. Then the question "how do we bring this into the platform?" naturally led to a full feature spec and 8-issue Linear project.

This is the dogfooding flywheel in action: using the product on a real customer → discovering a product gap → speccing the feature → building it → using it on the next customer.

But there's a more strategic observation: the report recommendations are essentially automated consulting deliverables. A traditional strategy consultant would spend weeks doing what the alignment engine did in minutes. The consolidated report is what a $50K McKinsey engagement produces — a prioritized list of strategic gaps with specific recommendations.

This has implications for positioning and pricing:
- For advisory firms (ScaleUp Company channel): the report is their deliverable, branded with their logo. They charge $20-50K, Stratafy charges $X/month.
- For direct customers (STIR channel): the report replaces a quarterly board strategy review. Instead of a consultant coming in once a quarter, the system runs continuously.
- For investors (Sequoia pitch): the report demonstrates quantifiable strategic improvement over time. "Our portfolio company went from 7.2 to 8.7 alignment in 90 days."

The consolidated report isn't just a feature — it's potentially the primary monetization mechanism for the advisory firm GTM channel.
