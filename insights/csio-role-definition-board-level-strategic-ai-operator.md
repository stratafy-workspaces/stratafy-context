# CSIO Role Definition: Board-Level Strategic AI Operator

## Summary
The AI-capable operator thesis requires a formal organizational role — Chief Strategy & Intelligence Officer with board representation. Every organization needs one. Stratafy's GTM shifts from selling software to defining a new role and providing its infrastructure.

## Description
Refinement of the AI-Capable Operator thesis. The role isn't just "person with AI tools" — it requires genuine organizational authority, likely at board level. Key refinements:

1. ROLE DEFINITION: Chief Strategy & Intelligence Officer (or similar) with board representation. Sits between CEO and operational leaders. Maintains the living strategic model, surfaces signals and risks, runs cross-domain analysis, ensures strategic coherence, acts as organizational memory.

2. EXPERIENCE REQUIREMENT: 15-20+ years cross-functional experience, systems thinker, AI-fluent, comfortable with ambiguity, strong enough to challenge CEO/board. This is a senior strategic hire, not a junior AI-savvy analyst.

3. UNIVERSALITY: Every organization of meaningful complexity will need this role — just as every organization eventually needed a CFO when financial complexity demanded dedicated capability. Strategic complexity has now reached the same threshold, and AI tools make it viable for one person.

4. GTM REFRAME: Stratafy isn't selling software — it's defining a new organizational role and providing the infrastructure. Companies don't buy "strategy software." They hire a CSIO and equip them with Stratafy. Platform adoption follows role adoption. This is a wedge that's much stickier than a software sale.

5. BOARD-LEVEL VALUE: This person provides boards with continuous strategic intelligence vs. quarterly backward-looking packs. For PE-backed companies especially, this transforms portfolio monitoring from periodic to persistent.

Builds on: Fractional Executive Channel Strategy → AI-Capable Operator Thesis → now this role definition layer.
