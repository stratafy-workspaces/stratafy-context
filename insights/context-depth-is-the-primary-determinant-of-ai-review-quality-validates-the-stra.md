# Context depth is the primary determinant of AI review quality — validates the Stratafy context moat

## Summary
Two Grok reviews with identical input but different lenses produced dramatically different quality. The difference wasn't the lens — it was the context available. This proves Stratafy's accumulated strategic context is what makes Perspective Reviews valuable, and that context compounds over time as a moat.

## Description
The contrast between STR-119 (Technical Advisor — high signal) and STR-120 (Startup Advisor — low signal) proves a critical product principle: AI review quality is directly proportional to the specificity and depth of context provided. The Technical Advisor had the full methodology and glossary to analyze, producing specific architectural gaps. The Startup Advisor had the same input but the lens required operational context (traction, funding, team composition, distribution strategy) that wasn't available, so it defaulted to generic startup playbook advice.

This validates that Perspective Reviews built inside Stratafy — with access to the full strategy tree, assumptions, risks, metrics, decisions, and execution state — will produce fundamentally different (and better) output than any external AI review. The context IS the moat. A competitor could copy the lens definitions, but they can't copy the accumulated strategic context that makes each review specific.

This also explains why advisory boards and consultant engagements often disappoint: they operate on limited context snapshots. Stratafy's Perspective Reviews will have continuous, comprehensive context — like the difference between a doctor who sees you once and your physician who has your full medical history.
