# Stratafy is three inseparable layers unified by one opinion — infrastructure, platform, operating system

## Summary
Stratafy = (1) first-principles strategy infrastructure with typed domain entities, (2) a platform of tools built on top, and (3) a continuous operating layer that keeps everything alive. The three layers only work together. The opinion about what strategy is — encoded in Layer 1 — is the moat that generic memory infrastructure cannot replicate from the bottom up.

## Description
Stratafy is not a single thing. It is three inseparable layers unified by one first-principles opinion about what strategy is.

Layer 1 — Infrastructure: A typed, domain-specific schema for how strategy actually works. Risks carry likelihood, impact, and scores. Assumptions carry confidence and impact-if-wrong. Decisions are Type 1 or Type 2. The hierarchy — Foundation → Strategy → Initiatives → Objectives/Metrics — is not configurable. It is the opinion encoded into the data model. Five intelligence layers (Risks, Assumptions, Decisions, Insights, Signals) wrap execution and form the feedback loop. No generic memory infrastructure can replicate this — it requires a specific, non-negotiable point of view about what strategy is.

Layer 2 — Platform: The tools, interfaces, and workflows built on top of the infrastructure. Chat, Alignment Scanning, Radar, Cortex, Story Mode, InsightSync, Pulse, Context Matrix, Graph, Finance, Organogram. Opinionated, but expressions of the infrastructure — not the infrastructure itself.

Layer 3 — Operating System: The continuous processes that keep everything alive without being asked. Distillation cron (change-aware, every 5 minutes). Health scoring. Content quality flags. Role-based context packaging. Permission governance. Staleness decay. Delta context. This is what separates Stratafy from a well-designed database with a good UI.

Why the layers are inseparable: infrastructure without the operating layer goes stale. A platform without the infrastructure competes on features. An operating layer without domain infrastructure has nothing meaningful to operate on. The three create the flywheel.

The Salesforce analogy: the opinion about how sales works, encoded in Salesforce's schema, is why it dominates. You cannot replicate that from a generic database up. Stratafy's thesis is that strategy needs its own Salesforce moment — and the opinion is not separable from the product.

Action required: this definition should be encoded as a belief or principle in the Foundation directly in the platform. It is too foundational to live only as an insight.
