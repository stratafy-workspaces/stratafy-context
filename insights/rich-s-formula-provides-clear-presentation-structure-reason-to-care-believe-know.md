# Rich's Formula provides clear presentation structure: Reason to Care → Believe → Know → Do

## Summary
Rob introduced Rich's formula for pitching: Reason to Care, Reason to Believe, What They Need to Know, What They Need to Do. The primary focus should be nailing "reason to care" — everything else follows. Applicable to all sales conversations, not just the EO forum.

## Description
Rich's formula provides a clean structure for any pitch or presentation:

1. Reason to Care — the problem and why it matters to THIS audience
2. Reason to Believe — proof it works (demos, data, testimonials)
3. What They Need to Know — the minimum concept to understand the solution
4. What They Need to Do — clear next step

Rob's emphasis: most pitches fail at step 1. If the audience doesn't care about the problem, nothing else matters. For Stratafy, the reason to care is not "AI is cool" — it's "your strategy dies the moment the offsite ends, and every decision your team makes without strategic context is a coin flip."

This formula applies beyond the EO forum — investor pitches, sales calls, coach conversations. Consider adopting it as the standard structure for all Stratafy messaging.

Action: Apply Rich's formula to the EO presentation. Test whether it improves engagement vs. the current structure.
