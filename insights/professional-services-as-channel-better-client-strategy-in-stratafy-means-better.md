# Professional services as channel — better client strategy in Stratafy means better service delivery

## Summary
Professional services firms (presentation companies, consultants, agencies, coaches) are natural Stratafy channels because having client strategy structured in Stratafy makes their own service delivery better. Missing Link proves this — better strategy data means better presentations.

## Description
Key insight triggered by the Missing Link case: any professional services firm that needs to understand a client's strategy to deliver their service well becomes a natural Stratafy distribution channel. Missing Link builds presentations — if the client's strategy is already structured in Stratafy, the presentations are sharper, the briefing process collapses, and the output is more strategically aligned. This is the exact same dynamic as coaches: the service provider has a self-interested reason to get their clients onto Stratafy because it makes their own work better. This generalises to consultants, agencies, accountants, and any advisor who needs strategic context from clients. The implication is that Stratafy's GTM isn't just coaches — it's the entire professional services ecosystem that orbits around strategy. Missing Link is the first proof of this broader channel thesis.
