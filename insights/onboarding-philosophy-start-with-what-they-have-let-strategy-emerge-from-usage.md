# Onboarding philosophy: start with what they have, let strategy emerge from usage

## Summary
The Missing Link implementation revealed a potential onboarding philosophy for all Stratafy customers: don't require a fully defined strategy before getting value. Start with what they already have (a victory condition, a mission statement, even just a goal), get the team engaging daily, and let the strategy build itself from the insights that flow in. This flips the traditional "define then execute" model into "engage then define."

## Description
During preparation for the Missing Link implementation (March 2026), a key product insight emerged: the onboarding process should not require customers to define a full strategy before they get value.

Traditional approach: Define everything (mission, vision, strategies, initiatives, metrics) → execute → hope it sticks.

Stratafy approach: Start with your north star (victory condition, mission, or even just a goal) → get the team checking in daily → the AI experts surface insights → the strategy builds itself from reality.

This emerged from the Missing Link context — they don't have a formal strategy, just a "victory condition." Rather than asking them to do a full strategy definition exercise before starting, the plan is to:
1. Capture the victory condition as the foundation
2. Get the team doing daily check-ins
3. Let insights accumulate from team engagement
4. Use those insights to progressively build out the strategy layers (strategies, initiatives, metrics, objectives)

This solves the time-to-define pain point elegantly: you don't need to carve out weeks for strategy definition. You start with what you have, and the strategy emerges from actual business reality rather than a whiteboard session.

This could become the standard Stratafy onboarding:
- Day 1: Foundation (what do you already know — victory condition, mission, key goals)
- Week 1: Team engagement begins (daily check-ins, first insights flowing)
- Week 2-4: AI experts start building strategy from accumulated intelligence
- Month 2+: Full strategic infrastructure in place, continuously evolving

The implication for PMF and positioning: "You don't need a strategy offsite to start. You need a starting point and a team that shows up. We'll build the rest together."

This also addresses the methodology frustration insight — people don't need another framework. They need infrastructure that works with whatever they already have.
