# Ask about unsolved problems, not feature ideas — friendly audiences give encouraging feedback, not truth

## Summary
Rob flagged that asking a friendly audience for ideas or feature requests yields encouraging but unhelpful feedback. Instead, ask "what real problems do you have that you don't have a solution for?" This surfaces the size and value of actual pain points rather than polite validation.

## Description
Rob Horton made a critical observation about customer discovery in friendly audiences (EO forum, advisor networks, friends): asking "what would you want from this tool?" invites people to be helpful and encouraging. They'll suggest features and say nice things. But this isn't real signal.

The better question: "What's a real problem in your business right now that you don't have a solution for?"

This shift does three things:
1. Surfaces genuine pain — the problems they actually lose sleep over
2. Reveals problem SIZE — how much time/money/energy the problem costs
3. Avoids solution bias — they describe the pain, not their imagined fix

For the EO forum specifically, this means reframing the Q&A section. Instead of "which feature would you use?" ask "where does strategy break down in your org?" and "what happens after your strategy offsite — does anything actually change?"

This insight applies to all customer discovery, not just the forum. Every demo, every sales call — lead with problem validation, not solution validation.

Action: Rewrite the EO forum Q&A questions to focus on unsolved problems rather than product feedback. Apply this principle to all customer conversations.
