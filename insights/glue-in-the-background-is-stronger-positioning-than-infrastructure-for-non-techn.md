# "Glue in the background" is stronger positioning than "infrastructure" for non-technical audiences

## Summary
Rob Horton independently framed Stratafy as "glue in the background" — staff interact through Chat, Telegram, Claude and never open Stratafy directly. This reframes adoption from "get people to use Stratafy" to "connect Stratafy to where people already work."

## Description
During pre-forum discussion with Rob Horton (EO forum member), he independently arrived at the "glue in the background" framing without prompting. This suggests the positioning resonates naturally with business leaders.

Key distinction: "Infrastructure" is technically accurate but abstract. "Glue in the background" is visceral — it immediately communicates that most users never need to open another app. The strategy flows into their existing tools.

This has implications for messaging at every level — investor decks, sales conversations, and the EO forum presentation. Consider using "infrastructure" for technical/investor audiences and "glue in the background" for operator/CEO audiences.
