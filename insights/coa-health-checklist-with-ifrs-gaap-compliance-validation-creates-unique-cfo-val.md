# COA health checklist with IFRS/GAAP compliance validation creates unique CFO value proposition

## Summary
Extending the COA health checklist to validate against IFRS and GAAP standards transforms the feature from strategy-only to compliance-grade. This earns trust with finance teams and bundles strategic alignment into a workflow CFOs already must do — making adoption pull-through rather than push.

## Description
The COA health checklist becomes significantly more compelling when it validates not just strategic alignment but also compliance alignment to IFRS and GAAP accounting standards. A single scan answering three questions simultaneously — Is your COA aligned to strategy? Is it structurally healthy? Is it compliant with your reporting framework? — creates a trifecta no single tool delivers. For CFOs, the compliance layer is what moves the feature from "interesting strategic tool" to "something my team needs." IFRS compliance checks include IAS 1 presentation, IFRS 15 revenue disaggregation, IFRS 16 leases, IFRS 9 financial instruments. GAAP checks include ASC 606, ASC 842, ASC 820, ASC 280, ASC 718, ASC 740. Multi-framework validation for organizations operating across jurisdictions is a further differentiator.
