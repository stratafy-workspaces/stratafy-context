# Perspective Reviews are Stratafy's "aha moment" feature

## Summary
Perspective Reviews may be the single feature that makes Stratafy's value proposition instantly tangible. "Run your strategy through 8 functional lenses" is understandable in one sentence. Every other feature requires explanation.

## Description
Today's session revealed that Perspective Reviews have a unique property among Stratafy features: they're instantly comprehensible. You can explain the value proposition in a single sentence — "A solo founder gets a virtual leadership team that stress-tests every strategic decision through 8 functional lenses" — and anyone who's run a company immediately understands what that means and why it's valuable.

Compare to other features:
- Alignment Scans: requires explaining what "alignment" means in context
- Radar: requires explaining the intelligence → triage → routing loop
- Decision Log: requires explaining why structured decision capture matters
- Recursive Flywheel: requires explaining compounding intelligence
- MCP/Agent architecture: requires explaining what MCP is

Perspective Reviews require zero conceptual overhead. Everyone knows what a CFO does. Everyone knows what a CTO does. "What would your CFO say about this strategy?" is a question any CEO has asked themselves. Stratafy just answers it automatically, continuously, with full context.

This makes Perspective Reviews the ideal:
1. Demo opener (run a live review during the demo)
2. Sequoia slide (one sentence, immediately resonant)
3. Advisory firm hook (this is what they do manually, now automated)
4. Free trial driver ("run one perspective review free, see what your virtual CFO finds")

Implication: Consider building Phase 1 of Perspective Reviews (STR-121) earlier than planned. It may be the most effective PMF validation tool available — if prospects react strongly to a live review on their own data, that's Sean Ellis territory.

Related: The Grok examples (STR-119, STR-120) serve as both positive and negative demonstrations that make the product case concrete.
