# Time to define strategy is the real barrier — both in session and after

## Summary
EO Forum members identified the time it takes to define a strategy as a core pain point — not just in the strategy session itself, but in turning it into something usable and maintained afterward. This maps to dragon #1 (No time) but is more specific: the bottleneck isn't just "being too busy" — it's that the process of defining, structuring, and keeping strategy alive is too heavy.

## Description
During the EO Forum Deep Dive (March 2026), attendees surfaced a specific pain point: the time required to define a strategy well. This showed up in two forms:

1. In the strategy session itself — the process of going from discussion to a structured, well-defined strategy takes too long. By the time you've captured everything, the energy is gone and the document is already incomplete.

2. After the session — turning a strategy session output into something that stays alive and useful is a separate, equally heavy task. Most teams don't do it, which is why the strategy doc dies within weeks.

This is more specific than the original dragon #1 ("No time — Strategy always loses to operational urgency"). The urgency here isn't about being too busy to think about strategy — it's about the process being too heavy even when you DO make time for it.

Stratafy addresses both sides:
- The structured layers (Foundation → Strategies → Initiatives → Metrics → Insights) provide a framework that makes defining faster — you're filling in a structure, not staring at a blank page
- The AI experts keep it alive afterward — no manual maintenance required
- This could be a powerful positioning angle: "Define your strategy in hours, not weeks. Keep it alive without lifting a finger."

This connects to the PMF framing insight — if the problem is "I tried to define my strategy and it took too long / died afterward," then Stratafy's value is immediately clear.
