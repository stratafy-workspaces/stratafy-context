# AI over-reliance risk: teams may chat with AI instead of executing — needs "guardian" and progress enforcement

## Summary
Rob raised concern that team members might spend excessive time interacting with AI rather than executing tasks. This needs two solutions: prompts designed to enforce progress (like "let's go"), and the Guardian of the Context role to review AI outputs. Amazon analogy: restricting junior devs from submitting unchecked code.

## Description
Rob Horton raised a practical concern: if every team member has access to AI strategy experts, what prevents them from spending all day chatting with the AI instead of actually doing their work?

This is a real adoption risk that will come up in sales conversations and at the EO forum. Two complementary solutions:

1. **Progress-enforcing prompts** — the "let's go" or "start your day" prompt is designed to set context, assign priorities, and then get out of the way. The AI should be a 5-minute morning check-in, not an all-day conversation partner. Design prompts that create urgency and move people toward execution.

2. **Guardian of the Context** — extends the previous insight. Not only does someone need to govern strategy updates, they also need to monitor usage patterns. If a team member is spending 2 hours/day in AI conversations, that's a signal — either they're stuck and need help, or they're procrastinating.

Rob drew an analogy to Amazon restricting junior developers from submitting unchecked code. The same principle applies: AI-proposed strategy changes need review gates proportional to their impact.

This concern will definitely surface at the EO forum. Have a crisp answer: "The AI is designed to be a 5-minute daily check-in, not a replacement for doing the work. It sets your priorities and gets out of the way."

Action: Design prompts with built-in time boundaries. Consider usage analytics that flag excessive AI interaction as a feature for team leads.
