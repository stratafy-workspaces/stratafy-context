# Four-Scan Strategic Intelligence (Intent + Capacity + Reality + Perception) with financial alignment as cross-scan dimension

## Summary
Four scans form the complete strategic intelligence picture: Alignment (Intent), Organigram (Capacity), Radar (Reality), Public Narrative (Perception). Each enriches the others in a compounding flywheel. Financial alignment integrates as a cross-scan dimension, not a fifth scan — preserving the clean framework while enabling a premium advisory module.

## Description
SYNTHESIZED FROM 2 INSIGHTS (Feb 12, 2026): 68c68fea, 98868e37

FOUR-SCAN FRAMEWORK: Intent (Alignment Scan) + Capacity (Organigram Scan) + Reality (Radar Scan) + Perception (Public Narrative Scan) = Complete Strategic Intelligence. Three inward-facing scans plus one outward-facing scan.

COMPOUNDING EFFECT: Each scan enriches the others. A radar finding about a competitor becomes actionable when you can check org capacity to respond. A public narrative gap becomes urgent when the organigram shows you've pivoted but haven't updated messaging. Perception drift detected by public scan can reveal strategy drift alignment scan hasn't caught.

FINANCIAL ALIGNMENT AS CROSS-SCAN DIMENSION: Financial data should enrich existing scans as an optional dimension rather than becoming a fifth scan. Preserves the clean 4-scan framework, avoids trust barrier of dedicated financial analysis.
- Alignment Scan: Does budget allocation match stated priorities?
- Organigram Scan: Is headcount investment aligned with strategy?
- Radar Scan: Does runway support the strategy timeline?
- Public Narrative Scan: Does pricing reflect positioning?

Financial data becomes an optional input that upgrades existing scans. Advisory firm angle: coaches already look at financials alongside strategy — this gives them a tool connecting the two. Premium module opportunity.

CATEGORY POSITIONING: "Continuous strategic intelligence across four dimensions — the nervous system that tells an organization whether it's aligned, capable, oriented to reality, and perceived correctly."
