# Energy and compute constraints could throttle or redirect AI adoption urgency

## Summary
Data centers doubling energy use by 2026, chip export restrictions fragmenting compute. Could slow AI adoption (reducing governance demand) or force disciplined deployment (increasing it).

## Description
IEA projects data centers will double energy consumption by 2026. 40% of data centers expected to use onsite power generation by 2030. US export restrictions on advanced chips to China creating geopolitical compute fragmentation. Goldman Sachs estimates $200B in AI infrastructure investment in 2025 alone. If energy/compute constraints throttle enterprise AI adoption speed, the urgency for governance tooling could soften — companies that can't scale AI don't need to govern it. Conversely, scarcity could accelerate the need for strategic prioritization of AI investments (which agents get compute, which workflows get priority), making Stratafy's strategic allocation role more critical. The net effect depends on whether constraints slow adoption broadly or just force more disciplined deployment. For Stratafy's positioning: frame governance as the tool that ensures scarce AI resources go to strategically aligned work first.
