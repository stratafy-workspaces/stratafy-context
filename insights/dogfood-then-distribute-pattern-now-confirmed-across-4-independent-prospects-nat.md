# Dogfood-then-distribute pattern now confirmed across 4 independent prospects — natural adoption motion

## Summary
GlobeScan is the 4th prospect to independently propose using Stratafy internally first, then rolling to clients. The pattern is natural, not coached. GlobeScan adds scale dimension — global firm, large corporate clients.

## Description
Bev Buchanan from GlobeScan independently proposed the same adoption pattern: use Stratafy for GlobeScan's own strategy first, then roll out to their clients. This is now the fourth independent data point of the "dogfood then distribute" pattern (after STIR, Nicholas/ScaleUp, Too Many Robots). Nobody is being coached into this path — prospects are arriving at it themselves. This suggests it's a natural adoption motion, not a sales construct.

What makes GlobeScan distinct is scale. This isn't a solo consultant or small firm — it's a global B Corp with 10 offices serving large corporates (Safaricom, Old Mutual, Barclays Africa, Exxaro). If even one office adopts and rolls to clients, the workspace multiplication effect is massive. This is the first signal that the advisory channel thesis could scale beyond boutique firms to mid-market professional services firms.

Also notable: Bev's expertise is in stakeholder engagement and organizational culture — she naturally thinks about alignment across stakeholders, which maps directly to Stratafy's alignment and foundation layers. The product-methodology fit is strong.
