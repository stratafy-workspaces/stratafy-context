# InsightSync and Pulse must remain architecturally distinct — session tool vs monitoring tool

## Summary
InsightSync = synchronous, multi-participant, session-based. Pulse = asynchronous, individual, recurring. Never conflate. Pre-execution readiness is InsightSync. Ongoing alignment tracking is Pulse.

## Description
InsightSync and Pulse are architecturally distinct and must remain separate products. InsightSync is synchronous, multi-participant, and session-based — it brings people together to work through something collectively. Pulse is asynchronous, individual, and recurring — it monitors each person independently and surfaces trends over time. Conflating them would degrade both. The pre-execution readiness check (previously considered a Pulse variant) is correctly an InsightSync session — it is a facilitated conversation between stakeholders, not a monitoring check-in. For the We Are 52 partner model, the full engagement arc maps cleanly: InsightSync (diagnostic intake) → InsightSync (pre-execution readiness, one week post-roadmap) → Pulse (ongoing weekly monitoring through execution). Two tools, three use cases, clean separation of concerns. Additionally, InsightSync is ad hoc and multi-purpose — it can be deployed at any strategic inflection point without a fixed cadence, making it a repeatable billable touchpoint for partners throughout an engagement lifecycle.
