# The real dragon: "I know I should use AI but don't know how"

## Summary
EO Forum members consistently expressed that they know they should be using AI in their businesses but don't know how to start. This wasn't on the original list of 9 dragons — and it may be the most urgent one. Stratafy's positioning could shift from "strategy execution infrastructure" to "the answer to how you start using AI meaningfully in your business."

## Description
During the EO Forum Deep Dive (March 2026), a theme emerged that was not part of the original presentation: most attendees said they know and feel they should be using AI, but aren't sure how to do this in their business.

This is significant because it represents a different kind of urgency than the 9 strategy execution dragons on the presentation. Those dragons are about strategy execution pain. This dragon is about AI adoption anxiety — the fear of being left behind combined with paralysis about where to start.

Stratafy may sit at the intersection of both: it gives AI the strategic context it needs to be useful, which means it's simultaneously a strategy execution tool AND the answer to "how do I start using AI in a way that actually matters for my business."

The positioning implication is potentially transformative:
- Old framing: "Strategy execution fails. Stratafy fixes it."
- New framing: "You know you need AI. Start with your strategy. Stratafy gives AI the context it needs to actually help."

This reframe connects to the Claude-native delivery insight — the audience is already exploring Claude/AI tools. They just don't know how to make it relevant to THEIR business. Stratafy is the bridge.

This dragon likely has more urgency than any of the 9 on the original list because it's driven by market pressure (everyone is talking about AI) rather than just internal operational pain.

Next steps: Test this framing in subsequent conversations and pitches. Watch for whether "how do I use AI in my business" generates more urgency than "my strategy execution is broken."
